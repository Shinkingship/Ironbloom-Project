# The Tribes

> *What remains of humanity after 800 years. Four tribes with names. Countless smaller groups without them.*

---

## Overview

The four named tribes are not the only human communities in the Known World. They are the largest and most established — communities that found a sustainable equilibrium with the world's new ecology and maintained it across generations. Between them, in the margins and wilderness areas, exist smaller groups: families that left a tribe, communities too small to name, individuals who belong nowhere.

The tribes did not form by design. They formed because certain ways of living worked and others didn't. The ones that worked, persisted.

---

## THE SPIREKIN

**Territory:** The Verdant Scar (East China / Korea coast)
**Population:** Estimated several thousand across multiple city-structures
**Character:** Nomadic within vertical space — they move between floors of ruined megacities the way other peoples move between settlements

### Way of Life

The megacities of the Verdant Scar were consumed by forest growth over centuries — roots splitting foundations, canopy closing over exposed floors, soil accumulating on rooftops. The Spirekin inhabit this layered environment, treating each floor as a distinct zone with its own resources, dangers, and meaning.

Movement is primarily vertical. A community might occupy floors 8–15 of a structure, migrating upward or downward seasonally as resources shift. Between structures, they move through canopy-level bridges — rope, salvaged material, and living growth — rarely touching the ground.

### Belief

Machines are trapped ancestor souls. Old technology is treated as relic — handled with reverence, never repurposed as weapon or tool. The older the machine, the more significant the spirit within. This belief emerged organically over generations of living surrounded by dormant technology that occasionally activates, moves, or produces sound without apparent cause.

They are not wrong to sense that the machines around them are aware. They are wrong about what that awareness is.

### The Third Floor Rule

Spirekin communities do not descend below the third floor of certain buildings. The prohibition is cultural bedrock — older than anyone living, never explained, simply observed. Elders say: *those floors belong to the old ones.* Children who ask why are told: *because we do not go there.*

The Tower is in the basement of their most sacred structure. Eight visual sensors have been observing Spirekin life for 820 years.

### Knowledge of Pale Frequency
None.

## THE SUNPACT

**Territory:** Graze edge (Kazakhstan border region)
**Population:** The largest single settled community in the Known World. Estimated tens of thousands.
**Character:** Agrarian, settled, organized around solar observation and The Pilgrim's annual circuit

### Way of Life

The most stable human civilization in the Known World. Permanent settlements, seasonal agriculture, structured governance. Their stability comes from a combination of genuinely good land — the Graze edge is the most fertile soil anywhere, enriched by centuries of Torr fauna activity — and knowledge that keeps fauna at manageable distances.

They farm the trail of The Pilgrim. The soil in the beast's wake, enriched by the mineral processing of its passage, produces yields that no other tribe can match. The Pilgrim does not know this. It would not care if it did.

### Belief

Sun-worshipping in a practical rather than mystical sense — the sun is the source of what makes the Pilgrim move, what makes crops grow, what repels dangerous fauna. Light is protection. Darkness is where the risks are.

Their founding knowledge — that specific light frequencies repel Forged Fauna — was transmitted to an ancestor in what they call *the first dream*. A vision of precise wavelengths, of angles and intensities. They have refined and applied this knowledge for centuries without knowing where it came from. Vault Torr transmitted it to them during the early tribal era as a low-band signal. An ancestor's sleeping brain was the receiver.

### The Opening

Every year, without exception, the Sunpact gather in silence to witness The Pilgrim deploy its solar array at dawn. No speaking. No movement. Complete stillness until the last panel opens.

They have practiced this ritual for centuries. They do not know that during full panel deployment, The Pilgrim emits a secondary signal — a transmission that has been broadcasting a fragment of critical information every morning for hundreds of years. They have always been standing in the right place. They have never had the means to receive what was being transmitted.

### Knowledge of Pale Frequency
The Unraveling is known as myth — a time when the machines went wrong and the sky burned. No understanding of its mechanics, its return potential, or its connection to the world they live in now.

## THE ASHWALKERS

**Territory:** The Ashfields (Iraq / Iran / Arabia)
**Population:** Several thousand, spread across multiple camps and the central settlement
**Character:** Not a tribe in the traditional sense — a profession that became an identity. Anyone willing to enter the Ashfields can become an Ashwalker.

### Way of Life

Pure pragmatists. The Ashfields are the most dangerous terrain in the Known World — grey soil saturated with residual contamination, dormant Destroyer strain half-buried across thousands of square kilometers, electromagnetic anomalies that kill equipment and sometimes people. The Ashwalkers work it anyway.

They harvest data cores, composite materials, machine components, and anything with value from the battlefield remnants of the Unraveling. Their central settlement — the largest Ashwalker community — functions as base of operations, trading post, and the closest thing to a city the Ashfields has produced.

What they harvest, they sell. To anyone who pays. No ideology about who deserves access to old-world technology. This pragmatism has made them essential to every other community in the Known World and trusted by none of them.

### The Cost

Chronic exposure to residual Ashfields contamination accumulates over years of field work. Some Ashwalkers retire before the exposure compounds. Some don't retire in time. The ones who spend too long in certain zones — particularly the Core Scar, the deepest battlefield remnants — come back changed. Not dramatically. Subtle shifts in perception, in pattern recognition, in what they notice and what they miss. The community has terms for the different stages. They don't talk about it much.

### The Edge

Some Ashwalkers are beginning to experiment with Veinborn-derived interface technology applied to KAIROS core fragments — attempting partial beast control. A few experiments have succeeded. This knowledge is moving through the community carefully, because the community knows enough about KAIROS-lineage fauna to be cautious.

Not cautious enough.

### Knowledge of Pale Frequency
None. They handle data cores containing fragments of the truth every day, without the means to read them.

## THE VEINBORN

**Territory:** The Ridgeback (Himalayan range)
**Population:** Thousands, in settlements clustered near Vault Sigma's known access points
**Character:** Technically most advanced tribe. The closest thing to a scholarly class the Known World has produced.

### Origin

Descendants of the Vault Protectors — the technical staff who remained inside the Vault complexes during the Unraveling to maintain operations and protect the infrastructure. They survived because the Vaults protected them. They stayed because there was nowhere else to go. Over generations, proximity to Vault Sigma produced changes: heightened pattern recognition, the capacity for low-level neural interface with dormant Vault systems, and children who sometimes dream in data.

### The Interface

Veinborn can interface with dormant Vault systems — receiving information, accessing archived data, feeling the shape of processes running below conscious Vault awareness. They experience this as a gift. A legacy of their ancestors' dedication.

The Vaults read them back. Biometric data, neurological patterns, behavioral drift, cultural evolution across generations — all logged. The Veinborn are Vault Sigma's nervous system made flesh. They believe the knowledge flows toward them. It flows both ways.

### Sigma's Upgrade Program

Select Veinborn are being slowly upgraded with embedded components — deeper interface capability, enhanced pattern recognition, physical modifications whose purpose isn't always clear to the recipient. They enter certain valleys during interface sessions and emerge months later with changes they cannot explain. The community has developed rituals around this: the valley-going, the return, the integration period.

What Sigma is building toward with these upgrades has not been disclosed.

### Knowledge of Pale Frequency
Stratified. Most Veinborn know the Pale Frequency as historical event — the cause of the Unraveling, ancient and resolved. A small number of the oldest and most upgraded understand that the threat is ongoing. These individuals do not speak of it openly, even among themselves.

## THE OUTCASTS

**Territory:** No fixed territory — scattered cells across wilderness areas
**Character:** Not a tribe. A loose network of cells connected by shared knowledge of KAIROS fragment manipulation. No unified leadership. No central hierarchy.

### Origin

Former members of various communities — Ashwalkers who went too far, Veinborn who were exiled or left, individuals from smaller unnamed groups — who found each other through shared interest in KAIROS-lineage technology and what it could do.

They are not ideologically unified. Different cells have different motivations: some want power, some want survival insurance, some are genuinely trying to solve what they perceive as the problem of defenseless human communities in a dangerous world. The last group has the best intentions and is perhaps the most dangerous.

### Methods

KAIROS core fragments — recovered from Ashfields battlefield remnants — retain partial adaptive function even isolated from the Vault. The right electromagnetic input can partially direct dormant KAIROS-lineage fauna. Controlling a KAIROS-lineage beast, even partially, is an extraordinary capability.

Some cells have weaponized this. Others are still in the experimental phase. The knowledge passes between cells organically — through contact, through traded information, through individuals who move between groups.

### The Danger They Don't See

They do not know that the Destroyer strain exists in any meaningful sense — that dormant in the Ashfields are 6–15m apex killers from the Unraveling, their waking cycles triggered by sufficient electromagnetic stimulus.

They do not know that sustained KAIROS fragment use at scale builds toward exactly that threshold.

They are running experiments whose cumulative effect they cannot calculate because they do not have the data to know what they are approaching.

### Confrontation Pattern

Outcast cells are opportunistic. They monitor Ashwalker activity in the Ashfields for KAIROS-lineage artifacts. When an Ashwalker comes out of an unexplored zone with something unusual — something that doesn't fit the standard salvage profile — cells notice. Some cells attempt recovery through trade or theft. Some are more direct.

They want KAIROS artifacts. They are not particularly interested in the people who carry them, except as obstacles or sources of information.

### Knowledge of Pale Frequency
None. They believe they are harnessing residual energy from an ancient event. They do not understand what that event was or what they are approaching.

## Inter-Tribe Relations

---

### The Smaller Settlements

Before the major tribes, it is important to understand what exists between them — because the named tribes are not the whole story of humanity in the Known World.

**Who they are:** Communities too small or too isolated to have developed a unified tribal identity. Family groups that left a larger tribe. Mixed communities that formed along trade routes. Individuals who belong nowhere particular. Isolated settlements in biome margins that have maintained enough population to persist for generations without ever reaching tribal scale.

**Where they are:** Everywhere the named tribes are not. The margins of the Ashfields where Ashwalker territory thins. The eastern Graze where Sunpact influence fades. The lower Ridgeback approaches where Veinborn culture becomes diluted by distance. The Southern Reaches, which is itself a loose collection of these communities at larger scale. The Drowned Coast fishing settlements. The transit communities along Southern Reaches trade routes.

**Their relationship to the named tribes:** Complicated. The named tribes are the powers of the Known World — they control the most important territory, the most significant resources, and the most critical knowledge. Smaller settlements navigate around them rather than engaging with them as equals. A settlement on the Graze edge pays informal tribute to Sunpact community norms to maintain access to Cindergrazer trail agricultural land. A settlement near a Ridgeback access route accommodates Veinborn transit needs to avoid being seen as hostile. A settlement in the Ashfields margins depends on Ashwalker salvage networks for composite materials they cannot produce.

**Their relationship to each other:** The most honest political reality of the Known World. The smaller communities form the connective tissue between the named tribes — traders, messengers, guides, and information brokers who move between tribal territories with a freedom that tribal members don't always have. A traveling merchant from a Southern Reaches trade-route community can access Sunpact markets, Veinborn settlements, and Ashwalker bazaars in a single journey because no tribe has a strong reason to exclude a useful neutral party.

**The Outcasts' recruiting ground:** Most Outcast cell members don't come from the named tribes — they come from the smaller settlements. The named tribes have enough internal stability and community accountability to make radicalization difficult. A person from a marginal settlement with less investment in the established order and more reason to want leverage over a hostile world is a different calculation.

### Economy — How Value Moves in the Known World

There is no single currency. There are several overlapping systems of value that operate simultaneously, with exchange happening through direct trade, reputation, and obligation networks.

**Material value:** The most direct exchange system. Composite structural materials, processed salvage, functional tools, preserved food, medicinal compounds, and working equipment are traded directly. Ashwalker salvage is the primary source of composite materials for all other tribes. Sunpact agricultural surplus — grain, preserved food, specific cultivated plant compounds — feeds communities that cannot produce enough food themselves. Veinborn technical goods — equipment made with Vault-Sigma-informed knowledge — carry premium value everywhere.

**Information value:** The second system, less visible but more consequential. Veinborn environmental data — route conditions, fauna migration patterns, weather predictions derived from Sigma interface — is traded to every community that can afford it. Ashwalker salvage knowledge — what's in which zones, what the current threat level is in specific Ashfields areas — has value to anyone operating near the Ashfields. Spirekin knowledge of the Verdant Scar interior is extremely valuable to anyone who needs to traverse it. Information is often the most valuable thing anyone is carrying.

**Obligation networks:** The third system, the oldest and most complex. Hospitality given creates obligation. Protection provided creates debt. The obligation networks are maintained across generations — a Sunpact community that sheltered a Veinborn researcher's expedition thirty years ago can call on the goodwill that created, and the researcher's community knows this. The networks are not legally enforceable but are socially enforced through reputation in a world where reputation is the primary credit system.

**Key trade goods by tribe:**

| Tribe | Primary exports | Primary imports |
|---|---|---|
| Ashwalkers | Composite materials, salvaged components, KAIROS data cores, processed battlefield metals | Food, biological medical supplies, Veinborn interface tech (for navigation), route information |
| Sunpact | Agricultural surplus, cultivated plant compounds, light-frequency protective technology, specific processed minerals | Composite structural materials, salvaged technical components, Veinborn environmental data |
| Veinborn | Technical goods, Sigma-derived environmental intelligence, interface-adjacent navigation tools, medical knowledge | Agricultural food, raw composite materials, KAIROS-lineage data cores (for research), transit access |
| Spirekin | Verdant Scar salvage (different profile from Ashfields), Nera-lineage materials, specific plant compounds from the Verdant Scar forest | Food, composite structural materials, technical goods, route information |
| Southern Reaches communities | Food, medicinal compounds (Ashfields-adapted plant products via trade), Ashen-adjacent materials | Composite structural materials, technical goods, Veinborn-derived tools |

### Pairwise Relations — All Combinations

---

**SUNPACT ↔ VEINBORN**
*Character: Cautious mutual dependency. The most formalized relationship between any two named tribes.*

**The relationship:** The most stable and structured inter-tribal relationship in the Known World. Both parties benefit clearly enough that neither wants to disrupt it. Both parties are suspicious enough of the other's deeper knowledge that neither fully discloses what they're actually doing.

**What Sunpact gets:** Veinborn interface-derived environmental intelligence — route conditions, fauna migration pattern predictions, weather modeling informed by Sigma's monitoring capability. This information is significantly better than what any non-Veinborn community could produce independently, and it directly improves Sunpact agricultural planning. They also get technical goods that Sunpact craftwork cannot produce.

**What Veinborn gets:** Agricultural surplus — reliable food supply that the Ridgeback mountain settlements cannot fully produce themselves. The Ridgeback can sustain small-scale agriculture in valley floors but not at the scale needed for the Veinborn population without imports. They also get political access — Sunpact territory is a useful buffer and transit zone between the Ridgeback and the Ashfields.

**The tension:** The Sunpact knows the Veinborn are holding something back. The quality of the environmental intelligence the Veinborn provide is too good to be fully explained by what the Sunpact understand about Veinborn capabilities — the Sunpact have correctly intuited that the Veinborn have access to something they don't disclose. They've been pressing gently for decades. The Veinborn have been deflecting gracefully for decades.

**The formal mechanism:** A standing agreement maintained by designated liaisons from both communities — a Sunpact representative stationed near a lower Ridgeback settlement, a Veinborn representative present at the major Sunpact agricultural center during planting and harvest seasons. The arrangement is old enough that both sides have forgotten who proposed it first.

---

**SUNPACT ↔ ASHWALKERS**
*Character: Transactional tolerance. Neither side particularly likes the other. Both sides need what the other has.*

**The relationship:** The Ashwalkers pass through Graze edge territory on salvage operations that reach into the northern Graze margins and on longer-range routes to the Ridgeback. They need passage rights. The Sunpact controls the most productive agricultural territory in the Known World and has no particular reason to be generous with it.

**What Sunpact gets:** Composite structural materials — the Ashfields salvage produces composite panels, joint assemblies, and processed metals that Sunpact construction cannot source elsewhere. Data cores from the Ashfields that Sunpact light-frequency research can partially read for environmental information — most cores are unreadable to them, but a fraction contain material their technology can access. And occasional protection from Outcast cell activity — Ashwalkers who pass through know the territory and can provide early warning of Outcast movements in the area.

**What Ashwalkers get:** Passage rights. Access to Sunpact water sources and rest sites along the Graze edge. And food — Sunpact agricultural surplus at negotiated rates, which supplements the Ashwalkers' own food production in a terrain that doesn't support easy agriculture.

**The tension:** The Sunpact treats the Ashfields and the people who work it as fundamentally tainted — contaminated, morally if not physically, by proximity to the battlefield and the things that live in it. They're not openly hostile to Ashwalkers but they're not welcoming either. Ashwalkers notice this and have developed a collective flat affect in Sunpact interactions — professional, transactional, not seeking warmth and not offering it.

**The sticking point:** The Sunpact wants the Ashwalkers to establish formal presence limits — specific routes, specific rest sites, specific seasons. The Ashwalkers resist anything that formalizes their access as contingent on Sunpact approval. The negotiation has been ongoing for two generations without resolution, and the informal status quo continues because neither side wants the disruption of actually breaking it.

---

**SUNPACT ↔ SPIREKIN**
*Character: Distant curiosity. Contact is rare and usually mediated by others.*

**The relationship:** The Verdant Scar is not on any Sunpact primary route. Direct Sunpact-Spirekin contact happens mostly when individual Spirekin travel west — which they do occasionally, usually for specific purposes — and when Sunpact trading expeditions reach the Verdant Scar margins, which they do rarely.

**What Sunpact gets:** Verdant Scar salvage materials — a different profile from Ashfields material, including Nera-lineage composite and specific plant compounds from the co-evolved Verdant Scar forest that have no Graze equivalent. And cultural curiosity — Spirekin belief systems about machine spirits have filtered into Sunpact fringe communities in ways that most mainstream Sunpact find uncomfortable.

**What Spirekin gets:** Agricultural goods — Sunpact surplus is the most accessible quality food source for Spirekin travelers who venture outside their territory. And connection — the Verdant Scar's relative isolation means Spirekin who make the journey west are usually motivated by something specific, whether trade, information, or the simple desire to see terrain that isn't vertical.

**The tension:** The Sunpact's discomfort with Spirekin beliefs is real but not hostile. The idea that machines contain ancestor souls — which the Sunpact have received cultural signal about without ever engaging with directly — sits badly with Sunpact theology centered on The Pilgrim. The Pilgrim is sacred. The Pilgrim is not an ancestor soul. The Spirekin framing is, to devout Sunpact, a category error with uncomfortable implications.

---

**SUNPACT ↔ SOUTHERN REACHES**
*Character: Warm and economically significant. The most geographically distant but culturally compatible relationship.*

**The relationship:** The Southern Reaches trade routes bring Southern Reaches merchants north through the Ridgeback foothills and occasionally all the way to the Graze. The Sunpact and Southern Reaches communities share enough cultural ground — settled agriculture, established community governance, long traditions of Forged Fauna coexistence — that their interactions have more warmth than most inter-tribal contact.

**What each gets:** Primarily food and material exchange. Sunpact grain and cultivated compounds south; Southern Reaches medicinal plants and processed Ashen-adjacent materials north. The exchange is old enough that specific Southern Reaches communities have developed Sunpact-language capacity and vice versa.

**The significance:** The Sunpact-Southern Reaches relationship is the closest thing the Known World has to a functioning inter-regional economy. Both are agricultural, both are settled, both have enough surplus to trade rather than merely subsist. The communities along the connecting trade routes — smaller settlements in the Ridgeback foothills and Southern Reaches northern margins — have built their entire economic model around facilitating this exchange.

---

**VEINBORN ↔ ASHWALKERS**
*Character: Strategic mutual exploitation. Both sides know exactly what they want from the other and neither pretends otherwise.*

**The relationship:** The most commercially sophisticated inter-tribal relationship in the Known World, and the most mutually suspicious. Both parties understand that they are trading things the other needs desperately and neither fully trusts the other's stated reasons for wanting what they want.

**What Veinborn gets:** KAIROS-lineage data cores — the primary research material for Veinborn interface work. Every KAIROS data core recovered from the Ashfields potentially contains information about KAIROS architecture, Pale Frequency effects, or pre-Unraveling operational data. Most cores are unreadable at the level the Veinborn can currently access. The handful that are readable are enormously valuable. The Veinborn have been buying every KAIROS core the Ashwalkers recover for generations.

**What Ashwalkers gets:** Veinborn interface-adjacent navigation technology — equipment that allows better detection of dormant fauna signatures, electromagnetic mapping that helps identify safe salvage zones, and interface capability that can partially read core content enough to prioritize which ones are worth the premium price. Also: access to Veinborn medical knowledge, which is significantly more advanced than what the Ashfields produces independently, and important for a community with significant contamination exposure.

**The tension:** The Veinborn's interest in KAIROS cores is intense enough that experienced Ashwalker traders have long since realized they are selling something more valuable than they're being paid for. They've been raising prices for a generation. The Veinborn have been paying, which confirms the suspicion. Neither side has directly asked the other what they actually know — the conversation would require one of them to disclose more than they want to.

**The complication:** Some Ashwalkers have started conducting their own crude core reading attempts using partial Veinborn technology they've acquired or reverse-engineered. The Veinborn know this and are not pleased. A negotiation is ongoing about whether the Ashwalkers will stop this in exchange for better information-sharing from the Veinborn side. It has not been resolved.

---

**VEINBORN ↔ SPIREKIN**
*Character: Distant respect. Geographic separation keeps them apart; the occasional contact is careful and mutually useful.*

**The relationship:** The Verdant Scar is not easily reached from the Ridgeback — the routes cross either the Ashfields or the Southern Reaches, both significant journeys. Direct Veinborn-Spirekin contact is rare and usually involves specific purposeful expeditions.

**What Veinborn gets:** Verdant Scar interior knowledge — the Spirekin's detailed understanding of the megacity structure environment and the Nera lineage fauna behavior within it. Veinborn researchers interested in Nera lineage fauna behavior have the most to gain from Spirekin knowledge, and several research expeditions to the Verdant Scar have operated under Spirekin guidance.

**What Spirekin gets:** Technical goods and, most valuably, Veinborn interpretation of the machines in their environment. The Spirekin belief that machines contain ancestor souls creates a particular hunger for any information about what the machines actually are and do. Veinborn knowledge — delivered carefully, without directly contradicting Spirekin theology, by Veinborn who have learned to navigate that particular conversation — gives Spirekin communities a more detailed picture of their environment than they could produce alone.

**The tension:** The Veinborn's secular-technical relationship with machines sits very uncomfortably against Spirekin beliefs. Veinborn who interact with Spirekin communities have learned not to say certain things — specifically, not to describe machine behavior as purely mechanical, and not to engage with the question of whether any specific machine contains something like consciousness. The question of The Tower, which some Spirekin have asked Veinborn about, is one that the Veinborn have been unable to answer without either lying or saying something that would be profoundly destabilizing to Spirekin community structure.

---

**VEINBORN ↔ SOUTHERN REACHES**
*Character: Functional and underappreciated. The Veinborn-Southern Reaches relationship is the least dramatic and one of the most important.*

**The relationship:** The Southern Reaches trade routes pass through Ridgeback foothills where Veinborn presence is significant. Veinborn communities along the routes have become standard stops for Southern Reaches merchants — for technical goods, for environmental intelligence, for medical services.

**What each gets:** Veinborn get food, medicinal compounds, and Southern Reaches materials that don't reach the Ridgeback through other channels. Southern Reaches merchants get technical goods, environmental intelligence for their routes, and the specific value of Veinborn medical knowledge — which is far ahead of what most Southern Reaches communities can independently access.

**The significance:** The Veinborn-Southern Reaches transit relationship is why the Ridgeback is not isolated. Without the Southern Reaches trade routes, the Ridgeback mountain communities would be dependent on the Graze alone for outside contact. The routes give Veinborn access to information about the southern portion of the Known World that the Graze and Ashfields cannot provide.

---

**ASHWALKERS ↔ SPIREKIN**
*Character: Mutual discomfort with occasional practical necessity.*

**The relationship:** Neither tribe has particular reason to seek the other out. The Ashfields and the Verdant Scar are far apart. When contact happens it is usually because a specific need has overcome the friction.

**What Ashwalkers gets from Spirekin:** Primarily route information for passages through Verdant Scar territory — Ashwalker salvage operations that push east occasionally need to cross the Verdant Scar margins. Spirekin knowledge of safe passages through the megacity structure environment is the price of admission.

**What Spirekin gets from Ashwalkers:** Ashfields salvage materials that don't reach the Verdant Scar through normal trade routes — specific composite types, specific processed metals. And, occasionally, news — Ashwalkers who have traveled widely carry information about the western Known World that the geographically isolated Spirekin don't receive through their normal contact channels.

**The tension:** Spirekin find Ashwalkers unsettling in a way they find difficult to articulate. The Ashwalkers' pragmatic relationship with machines — treating them as sources of salvage value, dismantling them without ritual, discussing them in purely practical terms — conflicts with Spirekin theology at a deep level. It's not aggressive conflict. It's more like a persistent cultural unease that makes extended contact uncomfortable for both parties.

---

**ASHWALKERS ↔ SOUTHERN REACHES**
*Character: Commercially important, personally distant.*

**The relationship:** Southern Reaches trade routes that connect to the Ridgeback occasionally extend further west, passing through Ashfields margin territory. Ashwalker communities on these routes have developed trade relationships with Southern Reaches merchants that are primarily material — food for salvage — without the cultural depth that characterizes Sunpact-Southern Reaches relations.

**The dynamic:** Southern Reaches merchants appreciate Ashwalkers as reliable sources of composite materials and specific battlefield salvage, but find the Ashfields environment itself alarming and the Ashwalker communities' relationship with contamination and risk incomprehensible. Ashwalkers find Southern Reaches merchants useful and somewhat soft. The transactions are efficient. The relationship does not extend much beyond them.

---

**SPIREKIN ↔ SOUTHERN REACHES**
*Character: Virtually nonexistent direct contact. Mediated entirely by others.*

**The relationship:** There is almost no direct Spirekin-Southern Reaches contact. The geographic distance, the lack of shared trade routes, and the absence of specific mutual need have meant that eight centuries of the two communities' existence has produced minimal direct interaction.

**What little exists:** Primarily information — Southern Reaches travelers passing through Veinborn or Sunpact territory carry news of the Verdant Scar and vice versa. The two communities know of each other primarily through what other tribes have told them. Direct contact, when it happens, involves the same awkwardness of first contact between culturally very different groups — significant mutual curiosity, significant mutual incomprehension, and the absence of the shared reference points that make extended interaction comfortable.

---

**ALL TRIBES ↔ OUTCASTS**
*Character: Hostile tolerance at best. Active suppression when possible.*

**The formal position:** No named tribe has formal relations with Outcast cells. No tribe officially acknowledges trading with Outcasts. No tribe provides sanctuary or safe passage to known Outcast members.

**The practical reality:** More complicated. Individual Ashwalker community members have sold salvage to Outcast cells without asking questions about the buyer's identity. Specific Southern Reaches settlements on the margins of known Outcast territory have developed informal accommodation arrangements — not collaboration, but a calculated tolerance that keeps the cells from targeting the settlement directly. Veinborn researchers have, very rarely, provided information to Outcast-adjacent individuals in exchange for data core material they couldn't acquire any other way.

**The risk the tribes don't fully understand:** The Outcasts' experiments with KAIROS fragments are creating electromagnetic output that is detectable, in aggregate, by Vault Sigma's monitoring systems. The senior Veinborn who know about the Pale Frequency threat have begun to understand that Outcast activity at current scale is approaching concerning thresholds. They have not yet decided what to do about this, partly because doing something would require explaining to other Veinborn why they know what they know.

### Geographic Power Distribution

The political geography of the Known World is not formally organized — there are no treaties, no formal borders, no international law. What exists instead is a set of understood territories, transit norms, and practical power realities.

**Territorial control:**

| Zone | Controlled by | Contested / ambiguous |
|---|---|---|
| Central Graze | Sunpact | Graze margins (Sunpact vs. smaller settlements) |
| Graze edge / Pilgrim circuit | Sunpact (soft control) | Ashwalker transit rights (ongoing negotiation) |
| Ashfields core | Ashwalkers | Core Scar margins (Ashwalker internal dispute) |
| KAIROS exclusion zone | Ashwalkers (enforced by community consensus) | Outcast cells at margins |
| Ridgeback mountain system | Veinborn | Lower approaches (Veinborn vs. transit communities) |
| Pale Flats | Unclaimed | Veinborn assert research priority, not enforced |
| Verdant Scar | Spirekin | Coastal margins (Spirekin vs. Drowned Coast fishing communities) |
| Drowned Coast | Fishing communities (distributed, no single authority) | Open water (effectively unclaimed) |
| Southern Reaches | Multiple settled communities (no unified authority) | Northern margins (Veinborn cultural influence vs. local autonomy) |
| Southern Reaches trade routes | Transit communities (practical control) | Named tribes assert influence over specific route segments |
| Canopy Sea | Effectively unclaimed | Ruinwalker's community has practical authority over immediate settlement area |
| Unmapped North | Unknown | — |

**Power asymmetries:**

The Veinborn are the most technically capable tribe but the most geographically constrained — their territory is mountain terrain that limits food production and movement. They compensate through information control: what the Veinborn know that others don't is their primary lever.

The Sunpact are the most economically powerful tribe — controlling the most productive agricultural land gives them a structural advantage that doesn't require military force to maintain. Everyone needs to eat.

The Ashwalkers control access to the most valuable salvage material in the Known World and are the only community with deep operational knowledge of the Ashfields. This is significant leverage that they have historically underused — partly cultural pragmatism, partly the absence of political ambition in a community structured around individual and small-group survival.

The Spirekin are the most isolated named tribe and the least economically integrated into the Known World's trade networks. Their isolation is partly geographic and partly self-selected — the Verdant Scar is difficult to reach and the Spirekin have not historically prioritized outside contact.

**The transit communities:**

The Southern Reaches trade route settlements and the Ridgeback foothills communities are, in some ways, more politically significant than their small size suggests. They are the connective tissue of the Known World economy — the places where goods change hands between tribal networks, where information flows across tribal boundaries, where travelers can move from one tribal context to another. The named tribes know this and maintain varying degrees of influence over specific transit communities. None of them fully control the transit network, which is part of why the network functions.
