### The Ashfields

**Real location:** Iraq / Iran / Arabia
**Tribe:** Ashwalkers
**Vault:** KAIROS (western edge, exclusion zone)
**Ancient One:** None confirmed

The Ashfields are what remains of the Unraveling's primary battlefield — the zone where the first KAIROS Destroyer strain emerged and where the most intensive fighting occurred over the decades before the signal faded. Eight hundred years of wind, minimal rainfall, and slow ecological recovery have produced a landscape of grey-brown soil compacted to near-stone density, broken by irregular formations of half-buried composite material from fauna that died here and were never reclaimed. The horizon is flat in most directions. The sky is large.

**The soil:** Not dead — contaminated in ways that most surface plant life cannot tolerate, with contamination profiles that vary sharply across short distances depending on what died where. Some zones grow nothing. Some grow dense, low, strange-colored vegetation adapted to the composite particulate. Some zones glow faintly at night from bioluminescent residue in the soil — the last trace of fauna that died here centuries ago. Ashwalkers know the glow zones by name. They do not camp in them.

**The terrain:** Flat in most areas, but not featureless. The Unraveling-era fighting produced impact craters, trenches, and depressions that have persisted through eight centuries of weather. Some are meters across; some are hundreds of meters wide. The largest craters collect rainfall and hold standing water for weeks, which draws fauna and creates temporary ecosystems around the edge.

**The Core Scar:** The most dangerous zone and the richest in salvage. A roughly oval area approximately 40km across in the western Ashfields where KAIROS and Vault fauna fought the last and most intense phase of the conflict. Destroyer strain density underground is higher here than anywhere else in the Known World. Soil contamination is severe enough to produce visible effects in humans within hours of direct exposure. Some Ashwalkers work the Core Scar margins regularly; the deep interior is avoided by all but the most experienced or desperate. Those who return from deep Core Scar work come back with salvage and something else — subtle changes in pattern recognition, in what they notice and what they don't. The community has words for the different stages of exposure. They use them neutrally, the way a community uses weather words.

**Destroyer strain distribution:** Distributed throughout the Ashfields, most concentrated in the Core Scar. Buried under centuries of soil accumulation, composite surfaces covered in mineral deposit indistinguishable from rock formations to untrained eyes. Experienced Ashwalkers know the signs: specific electromagnetic interference in equipment, unusual soil temperature gradients, the way certain fauna avoid specific areas regardless of apparent conditions.

**Vault KAIROS:** Located in the western Ashfields. Not visible — the Vault structure is buried, access points sealed. The 15km exclusion zone is maintained by Ashwalker community consensus based on accumulated casualty records. Equipment fails unpredictably near the Vault. Fauna behavior in the zone is anomalous.

**The settlement:** The central Ashwalker community — several thousand individuals — occupies a series of structures in the eastern Ashfields where contamination levels are manageable. Not a permanent structure in the architectural sense — more an arrangement of salvaged material and older human-era buildings that has accumulated over generations into something that functions as a base. Outsiders are welcome with something to trade. They are watched carefully until they demonstrate they understand the basic rules.

**Travel:** Navigable but not comfortable. Established routes between the settlement and major salvage zones are known to experienced Ashwalkers and not shared freely. Water sources are scarce and must be checked before use. The flat terrain that makes navigation easier also makes shelter from severe weather harder to find.

### The Graze

**Real location:** Kazakhstan / Mongolia steppes
**Tribe:** Sunpact
**Vault:** TORR (central Graze, buried)
**Ancient One:** The Pilgrim

The Graze is the most ecologically recovered biome in the Known World — a vast grassland with eight centuries of consistent Torr lineage processing. The result is a landscape of extraordinary soil quality and biological productivity: grasses that grow thick and fast, ground that holds water efficiently, mineral content that supports agriculture at yields no other region can match. The Sunpact did not create this. They were born into it.

**The landscape:** Flat to gently rolling, broken by occasional river valleys and the remnant infrastructure of pre-Unraveling human settlement buried under centuries of grass and soil. Some buried structures create subtle variations in terrain — slight rises, depressions where the grass grows differently. Sunpact farmers know which depressions flood, which rises are hollow, which lines in the grass follow buried walls. This knowledge is the foundational practical geography of Sunpact life.

**The grass:** Eight centuries of co-evolution with Torr lineage fauna has produced grass that doesn't exist anywhere else. Roots deeper. Growth rate faster. Recovery from Cindergrazer hoof impact within days. Some varieties have incorporated composite particulate into stem structures — harder, more drought-resistant than any botanical precedent. The grass is a product of the same ecological process that produced the fauna.

**Cindergrazer migration:** Three primary migration routes cross the agricultural zones with timing predictable to within a few weeks. The soil in the herds' wake — enriched by hoof processing, Millgrazer follow-up, and Seedwing shed particulate — produces the highest-yield agricultural land in the Known World. Sunpact farmers plant in the wake, harvest before the next passage, and have done this for as long as the oral record extends.

**Torr's buried sensor network:** The magnetic interference anomaly in the deep Graze — an area where compasses fail and salvage equipment produces anomalous readings — is caused by thousands of buried Torr monitoring nodes still transmitting to a Vault too dormant to fully process what they send. The anomaly zone covers roughly 200km² of the central Graze. Sunpact communities within it use landmark navigation and warn outsiders.

**The Pilgrim's circuit:** Annual. Takes approximately 11 months. The route varies slightly year to year — drought pushes it north, wet years pull it south. Predicting the variation is a significant element of Sunpact agricultural planning.

**The Opening:** Dawn, in the Pilgrim's presence. The entire Sunpact community within reach stops. No speaking. No movement. The Pilgrim deploys its 44 panels over several minutes — the only sound is the gold-copper filament fringe vibrating in whatever wind there is. When the panels begin closing, people start moving again without a word. Children who ask why are told: *because it is the new year.* Why silence is required, nobody explains. It is simply how it is done. During full deployment, the secondary signal transmits Core 2. The Sunpact has always been standing in the right place. They have never had the means to receive it.

**Vault Torr:** Buried beneath the central Graze. Sunpact tradition designates the access points as sacred ground — not entered, not disturbed. The Sunpact don't know it's a Vault. They know somewhere under there is something old and important. They are not wrong.

**Travel:** The easiest travel terrain in the Known World — flat, established routes, adequate water from river crossings. Outsiders who follow Sunpact protocol are received with cautious hospitality.

### The Pale Flats

**Real location:** Tibetan Plateau / Gobi Desert
**Tribe:** None permanent
**Vault:** None — the Array is not a Vault
**Ancient One:** The Listener

The highest accessible terrain in the Known World. Cold at all elevations, genuinely dangerous at altitude without preparation. The electromagnetic clarity here is unlike anywhere else on the continent — a function of altitude, low humidity, and near-absence of contamination particulate. Signals transmit further and more clearly from the Pale Flats than anywhere else.

The Pale Frequency arrives here first.

**The terrain:** Two zones. The lower Gobi margin — desert, cold nights, hot days, sparse fauna, minimal human use. The plateau proper — vast, cold, wind-scoured, altitude high enough to require acclimatization, horizon seeming slightly curved. Rock and thin soil, occasional glacial lakes, and the buried remnants of pre-Unraveling infrastructure that served whoever operated up here.

**Electromagnetic activity:** Equipment behaves differently on the Pale Flats. Veinborn researchers report that neural interface conditions change at altitude — more data, less noise, connections that take effort at lower elevations arriving without effort here. Some Veinborn have extended stays specifically to use the enhanced interface conditions. A few have stayed too long and come back changed in ways difficult to describe precisely.

**The Listener:** Present on the plateau. The merged-with-rock formation that has not moved in 180 years, sensor protrusions covering its entire visible surface. In right wind conditions, the tonal sequences carry across significant distances. Most travelers interpret this as unusual wind phenomenon. Veinborn researchers who have spent time near The Listener describe progressive coherence in the patterns — something emerging from what initially sounds like complex noise. None have decoded it. The dish formation orients skyward — has been oriented skyward for 180 years. The Eye pulse rate and pattern change in response to specific electromagnetic inputs from above. Whether it is also responding to something beyond the solar system is a question no one has publicly committed to.

**The Array:** See separate section below. Location known to Vault Sigma and contained in Core 4. No human faction currently knows the precise coordinates.

**Fauna:** Sparse. Frostgrazer at lower plateau ranges. Spindrift trails more visible here than anywhere else due to atmospheric clarity. Galethreader reported in severe weather at plateau edges.

**Travel:** Difficult. Altitude sickness affects unacclimatized travelers. Cold exposure is serious at plateau elevations even in summer. Magnetic and electromagnetic anomalies interfere with navigational equipment.

### The Ridgeback

**Real location:** Himalayan range
**Tribe:** Veinborn
**Vault:** SIGMA (multiple access points along the range)
**Ancient One:** The Listener (plateau edge)

The mountain spine of the continent. The Ridgeback bisects the Known World roughly east-west — northern plains on one side, southern subcontinent on the other. Veinborn settlements concentrate along the southern and western faces where Vault Sigma's access points are located.

**The terrain:** Extreme vertical variation. River valleys at lower elevations — temperate, forested, most livable areas, where most Veinborn settlements sit. Mid-range is rock and sparse vegetation, Stoneback and Cliffwarden territory. High range is Frostgrazer and Ironwing territory. Above that, permanent snow zone.

**Veinborn settlements:** Distributed along the mountain system in communities of varying size, connected by established high-altitude routes. Largest communities are near the most accessible Vault Sigma interface points — also the communities with highest interface rates and most concentrated technical knowledge. Smaller settlements further from access points have lower interface rates and more mixed heritage from generations of absorbing individuals from other tribes.

**Vault Sigma access:** Multiple points provide interface access. Some are well-known and regularly used — marked in Veinborn geography as sacred sites with specific approach protocols. Others known only to senior researchers. The interface experience varies by point: some provide general environmental data, some targeted research access, and some — the ones that senior Veinborn approach alone and don't discuss afterward — provide the deeper exchanges that result in the upgrade program.

**The valleys:** Some Ridgeback valleys are normal. Some are not. Near deep Sigma access points: equipment behaves differently, navigation requires more care, and some individuals experience *valley dreams* — vivid data-dense experiences during sleep that feel less like dreams and more like receiving a transmission. Veinborn who have experienced valley dreams are tight-lipped about the content. Some emerge from extended valley stays with new interface capabilities they cannot explain. The community treats this as normal. It has been normal for eight generations.

**The upgrade emergence:** Selected individuals enter specific access valleys alone for weeks to months. They emerge with modifications they cannot fully account for — new interface capabilities, enhanced pattern recognition, occasionally physical modifications at interface sites. The community has developed cultural frameworks around the emergence: what to expect, how to support integration, which modifications indicate which levels of Sigma engagement. The framework is calm and practical. What it is a framework for is extraordinary.

**Travel:** Transit point between Graze and Canopy Sea for east-west travelers. Ashwalker salvage teams pass through. Sunpact traders make the mountain crossing two to three times per year. Veinborn communities are valuable stops — technical knowledge and Sigma-derived environmental information make them useful for any significant journey.

### The Verdant Scar

**Real location:** East China coast / Korea
**Tribe:** Spirekin
**Vault:** NERA (coastal zone)
**Ancient One:** The Tower

Former megacities of the east coast consumed by forest over eight centuries. The result: vast building structures — some still standing to 40, 50, 60 floors — embedded in forest canopy, with root systems splitting foundations and forest growth colonizing every surface that holds soil. The Spirekin live in this environment the way other peoples live in forests, treating the vertical dimension of the buildings as their terrain.

**The structures:** Pre-Unraveling megacity construction. Mostly steel and composite, which has degraded much more slowly than organic construction would have. The tallest surviving structures are the most overbuilt original designs — engineering that was excessive for its era and has proved sufficient for eight centuries. Shorter structures have fared worse — partial collapses are common, and Spirekin maintain detailed knowledge of which structures are stable and which are not.

**The forest:** Not the forest that grew here before the Unraveling. Eight centuries of Nera lineage activity combined with unusual soil conditions of building rubble and composite decay has produced plant communities that match nothing from pre-Unraveling records. Trees grow through floors. Root systems split foundations and develop composite-incorporated structures at contact points. The Verdant Scar forest is co-evolved with the constructed environment it has consumed.

**Vertical life:** Spirekin communities occupy floor ranges within structures the way other communities occupy neighborhoods. A building's floors 8–22 might be home to one community; floors 30–45 to another. Between buildings, canopy-level bridges — rope and salvage and guided vegetation — connect adjacent structures. The community's effective geography is three-dimensional in a way that ground-dwelling tribes cannot easily navigate without a guide.

**The third-floor rule:** No Spirekin community descends below the third floor of certain buildings. Categorical — not *"be careful below the third floor"* but *"we do not go there."* Applied to buildings identified as having the wrong quality in the lower floors — a slightly different acoustic environment, a different smell, a temperature that doesn't match. The Tower is in the basement of the most sacred building, which is also the one with the strictest prohibition. Children who ask why are told: *because we do not go there.*

**The Tower's building:** The most sacred Spirekin structure, treated with reverence the community cannot fully explain. The building has unusual properties — better drainage, structural stability maintained without repair, controlled forest growth in and around it. All attributable to The Tower's presence and passive maintenance from below. The Spirekin do not know this.

**Travel:** Navigating the Verdant Scar without a Spirekin guide is strongly inadvisable. Ground level between structures is disorienting, structurally hazardous, and poorly understood — the Spirekin themselves rarely use it. Legitimate travel is via introduction to a community and navigation through their known routes.

### The Canopy Sea

**Real location:** Indochina / Thailand / Myanmar
**Tribe:** None established — Ruinwalker's community is the only significant human presence
**Vault:** LYSS (deep below northern Thailand)
**Ancient One:** Ruinwalker

The most biologically complex terrain in the Known World. A mega-jungle with eight centuries of Lyss lineage processing — more species interactions per square kilometer than any other biome, plant and fauna communities co-evolved into relationships that didn't exist before the Unraveling, ecological vitality that makes every other biome seem sparse by comparison.

**The canopy layers:** Four distinct vertical zones. The forest floor — dark, humid, high contamination at soil level, Rootwarden territory. The understory — filtered light, Tanglewarden and forest floor fauna, occasional ground-level transit for Ruinwalker's community. The mid-canopy — Ruinwalker's settlement, Deepcanopy moving at geological pace, KAIROS-lineage fauna operating. The upper canopy — the closed ceiling, where Canopy Mantis hunts.

**The water:** Ghost water. Driftspore colonies dense enough that most standing and slow-moving water shows the characteristic blue-green shimmer. Safe to drink directly from most sources. The Ruinwalker community has never needed significant water treatment practices. They know the shimmer and the quality are connected. They don't know why.

**KAIROS-lineage fauna:** Concentrated in the mid-canopy and understory. They move with what observers consistently describe as deliberateness — not instinctual, but collecting information. The Ruinwalker community has lived alongside them long enough to read specific behavioral states — what positions indicate passive, what movements indicate active interest, what to do when one approaches the settlement.

**Vault Lyss:** No surface access. Location somewhere below the northern Canopy Sea, depth unknown. The only evidence of its presence is the nature of the ecosystem above it — the degree to which Lyss fauna have evolved beyond design, the unusual quality of the forest directly above its estimated location, and the Veinborn description of Lyss interface as *"remembering something you were never told."*

**Ruinwalker's settlement:** Mid-canopy, 20–30m above ground. Approximately 200 people. Structures grown into and around the canopy, platforms and bridges with accumulated moss and small plant growth. Not a tribe — people arrive, stay, become part of it. The one constant is Ruinwalker, who has been here longer than the settlement itself has existed.

**Travel:** Reaching the Canopy Sea requires crossing either the Southern Reaches or the Verdant Scar. Internal navigation without local knowledge is challenging. The KAIROS-lineage fauna presence means that travelers who behave like threats face a different category of risk — not because the KAIROS fauna are aggressive, but because their response to threat stimuli is less predictable than other fauna's.

### The Drowned Coast

**Real location:** Philippines / Taiwan / Japan / Pacific coast
**Tribe:** Drowned Coast fishing communities — not a named tribe, multiple small settlements
**Vault:** TIDAL (South China Sea, submerged 40m)
**Ancient One:** Stillwater

Sea levels rose over the centuries following the Unraveling. Former coastlines are underwater. Former cities are partially submerged. The new coastline is ragged, cut through with channels between submerged structures. Underwater visibility in the shallower zones reveals a landscape frozen at the moment of submersion.

**The submerged cities:** Former coastal megacities now sitting in 5–30m of water. Upper floors still above surface in some areas; lower floors colonized by marine fauna and covered in Shellmass colonies. The towers that still stand above surface are used as navigation landmarks, observation platforms, and housing — the above-water floors of stable structures, reached by boat.

**The fishing communities:** Small settlements distributed along the coast, each centered on a boat-capable community working specific fishing zones. Not politically unified — each settlement has its own leadership and territorial fishing zones. They share practices and cultural references specific to coastal life without having a single tribal identity. Outsiders are assessed as potential trade partners.

**Marine fauna:** The Drowned Coast is Tidal territory in the water and Nera territory on the coast. Riftjaw and Deepform in the marine zone. Tidecrawler most commonly encountered at coast. Shellmass visible below surface in every shallow area. Skimveil observed as surface shadow. Harborform is the light in the estuary mud at night. These fauna are part of daily reality, understood empirically across generations.

**Stillwater:** Surfaces in the Philippine Sea zone. Surfacing events preceded by deep blue-green light from depth, then the leading edge of the 85m body breaking surface, water draining for minutes afterward. Fishing communities within sight observe from elevation. No boats approach. The protocol is universal — no community records why it started. It is simply what is done.

The bioluminescent pulse patterns have been mapped by Drowned Coast communities for generations. The maps exist as navigational records, dream interpretation guides, and cultural artifacts. They have never been decoded. The sequences repeat. The communities know Stillwater is saying something to something. They have given up expecting to understand it in a human lifetime.

**Travel:** Boat travel is the primary mode within the Drowned Coast. Ground routes between coastal settlements are limited by the terrain — channels, submerged sections, unstable building remnants. Community-to-community travel is almost entirely by water.

### The Southern Reaches

**Real location:** Indian subcontinent
**Tribe:** Multiple unnamed settled communities
**Vault:** ASHEN (Northern India, geothermal zone)
**Ancient One:** None confirmed on surface

The most populated biome in the Known World. Warmer climate, reliable monsoon rainfall, relatively lower contamination levels, and eight centuries of established human-fauna coexistence have allowed communities to develop at higher densities than elsewhere. The relationship between Southern Reaches communities and the Forged Fauna around them is the most complex of any non-Veinborn human group.

**The communities:** Not a single culture or tribe. Multiple distinct settled communities with their own languages, practices, and histories. What they share is ecological context — the same fauna, the same Ashen underground activity, the same long tradition of coexistence. Travelers from other biomes report that the Southern Reaches feel qualitatively different: more people, more cultivation, more accumulated cultural complexity.

**Fauna coexistence:** The most developed practical understanding of Forged Fauna behavior of any human group outside the Veinborn and Ruinwalker community. Not technical — empirical, accumulated across generations, transmitted as everyday cultural knowledge. Cavewarden is known by several local names and treated as a cave guardian. Tunnelworm emergence during geological activity is understood with specific predictive signs. Coldmantle seasonal migrations through the higher terrain are tracked as weather indicators.

**Ashen underground activity:** Tunnelworm networks are densest here — the geothermal activity near Vault Ashen drives higher burrowing fauna density. Southern Reaches communities feel this as occasional vibrations that don't match weather or earthquakes, unexpectedly clean water sources, agricultural soil that drains and holds moisture better than terrain should allow. They attribute these things to various local explanations — the land being well, good ground, old roots — without knowing what produces the effects below them.

**The Deep One legends:** Every Southern Reaches community within range has the legend. A very large something surfaces during significant earthquakes — the drill-head visible, the bioluminescent amber-gold lines along a pale white body, moving with purpose, gone before dawn. Consistent across communities that have had no contact with each other. The Veinborn, who have Vault Sigma's geological records, know the triangulated location of Vault Ashen and know what the legend is describing. They have not shared this.

**Trade routes:** The Southern Reaches are the primary overland transit between the Ridgeback and the Canopy Sea. Established routes maintained by communities along them. Communities on the routes have developed sophisticated hospitality and trade practices around transit traffic.

### Unmapped North

**Real location:** Siberia and Arctic zone
**Tribe:** Unknown
**Vault:** None confirmed
**Ancient One:** None confirmed

Beyond the knowledge of any tribe. The Pale Frequency hits the poles hardest — electromagnetic intensity is highest at the poles during signal transit. Whatever KAIROS-lineage fauna survived here experienced the Unraveling most severely. Whether any KAIROS fauna survived the polar Unraveling, or what they became if they did, is unknown.

The fauna that have evolved in the Unmapped North in isolation for 800 years are entirely undocumented. The cold alone would have driven significant evolutionary pressure. Without any Vault guidance, they have adapted to circumstances no Vault designed them for.

The Pale Frequency Array picks up electromagnetic signatures from the Unmapped North that suggest large-scale electromagnetic activity — consistent with large fauna populations, active geological processes, or something else. The signatures change over time in patterns that are not geological.

What exists in the Unmapped North is one of the genuine unknowns of the era.

## The Pale Frequency Array

**Location:** Pale Flats, Tibetan Plateau — coordinates known to Vault Sigma, contained in Core 4
**Original function:** Monitoring — the most sensitive electromagnetic receiver in PROJECT FORGE's continental sensor network
**Current state:** Dormant. Structurally intact. 800 years of low-maintenance and occasional weather damage have produced some degradation, but the core architecture is functional.

### Physical Description

Not a single structure but an installation — a distributed network of sensor elements across approximately 3km² of plateau surface, with a central processing and transmission hub.

**The sensor field:** Hundreds of receiver elements distributed across the installation area. Each element is a composite spire, 2–4m tall, with a composite dish or array structure at the top. Dark grey to near-black, weathered to a slightly roughened surface over 800 years. From a distance across the plateau, the installation looks like an unusual geological formation — too regular to be natural, but not immediately recognizable as constructed. From within, the regularity is obvious: spacing precise, orientation consistent, geometry deliberate.

**The central hub:** A larger structure at the installation's center — roughly 15m tall, broader at the base. Surface shows repair work from different periods, some done by PROJECT FORGE automated maintenance systems that continued operating for an unknown period after the Unraveling before stopping. Access points around the base are sealed — composite panels with mechanical locks not opened in 800 years. The interior contains signal processing hardware, power systems, and the transmission architecture that could convert the installation from receiver to transmitter.

**The condition:** Sensor elements largely intact — simple composite structures weather well. The central hub has surface damage from 800 years of Tibetan Plateau weather but core systems are functional per Vault Sigma monitoring. Power systems in low-power standby, drawing minimal charge from solar collection elements in the hub's surface. Not active. Not dead.

**The surroundings:** Specific fauna avoid the installation area — the low-level electromagnetic emissions from the dormant Array have established a behavioral boundary over generations. The cleared zone is not absolute but fauna density within the installation boundary is consistently lower than surrounding terrain. Spindrift trails are denser above the Array than elsewhere on the plateau.

### Access and Reactivation

No human faction currently knows the precise location. Coordinates in Core 4 and Vault Sigma's restricted archives.

Reaching the Array requires crossing the Pale Flats — significant preparation, altitude acclimatization, navigation without reliable equipment in some zones.

Accessing the central hub requires opening the sealed access points — mechanically possible with appropriate tools, not requiring special authorization. The sealing was protective, not security.

Reactivating and recalibrating the Array requires:
- Physical presence at the transmission control systems inside the hub
- Sufficient understanding of the Array's architecture to correctly configure transmission parameters
- The precise counter-signal frequency specifications from Core 5
- Vault Sigma guidance during calibration through available Veinborn interface

The recalibration cannot be done by following instructions without understanding them. The margin for error in counter-signal frequency is narrow enough that miscalibration produces worse outcomes than no transmission. The operator must understand *why* each parameter is set the way it is, not just *that* it should be.

This is why Vault Sigma insists the human who activates the shield must genuinely understand what they are doing. It is not philosophy. It is engineering reality.

## Beyond the Known World

Australia, Europe, the Americas — exist as mystery only. No tribe has confirmed contact with anything beyond the continent. Data cores occasionally contain geographic records that don't match any known land. The Pale Frequency Array picks up secondary echoes bouncing from distant sources.

PROJECT FORGE deployed fauna globally before the Unraveling. KAIROS-lineage fauna were among the most widely distributed. On every other continent, these lineages have been evolving in isolation for 800 years — without Vault guidance, without contact with Known World populations, without the shield.

The echoes from beyond the Known World are not random. They repeat. They have structure. Someone — or something — is producing them deliberately. The pattern does not match any Known World signal type. It does not match any Vault communication protocol. It does not match fauna electromagnetic output as understood from Known World documentation.

Whatever is producing the echoes knows the Array is listening.

## Flora

Eight hundred years of Forged Fauna activity has fundamentally altered plant ecosystems across the Known World. The vegetation that exists today is not the vegetation that grew here before the Unraveling — shaped by eight centuries of co-evolution with biomechanical organisms that process soil chemistry, clean water, alter atmospheric composition, and in some cases directly incorporate composite material into the growth substrate.

### General Patterns

**Composite incorporation:** In areas of high fauna activity and high composite material availability — particularly the Ashfields margins and the Verdant Scar — some plant species have incorporated composite particulate into their structural tissues. Measurable but not visually dramatic: stems and root systems harder, more chemically resistant, more drought-tolerant than the same species elsewhere.

**Processing residue utilization:** Plants near active Forged Fauna processing zones often grow faster and larger than the same species elsewhere. The chemical byproducts of fauna processing — inert residue compounds — turn out to be useful mineral inputs for plant growth. The relationship is not designed symbiosis; it is an emergent consequence that plants have, over eight centuries, adapted to exploit.

**Bioluminescent transmission:** In the Canopy Sea and Verdant Scar, some plant species in areas of high Seedwing or Driftspore activity have developed faint bioluminescent properties. Too faint to be visible in daylight but creates a shimmer in dense vegetation at night in these areas.

### Biome-Specific Notes

**The Graze:** The grass is the primary flora story. Co-evolved with Torr lineage fauna over eight centuries — deeper roots, faster growth, faster recovery from hoof impact. Some cultivated Sunpact varieties have incorporated composite particulate from Millgrazer processing into stem structures. Treated as premium varieties; seed stock carefully maintained.

**The Verdant Scar:** The most dramatic flora development in the Known World. Forest that has consumed the megacities has incorporated composite material from 800 years of structural decay and fauna processing. Some large tree species in the deepest Verdant Scar have root systems that cannot be distinguished from manufactured composite structures at their cores — organic tissue has grown around and through composite remnants until the two are integrated. These trees are extraordinarily stable and long-lived.

**The Canopy Sea:** Entirely distinct from pre-Unraveling tropical forest. Species interactions that don't exist anywhere else — plants that appear to have developed relationships with specific Lyss lineage fauna over centuries. The Canopy Sea flora cannot be fully understood without the fauna, and vice versa.

**The Ashfields:** Sparse, strange-colored, chemically distinct. Species adapted to contamination profiles toxic to plants elsewhere. Some Ashfields vegetation produces compounds as metabolic outputs directly useful as medicinal or industrial inputs — a consequence of processing contamination chemistry rather than normal soil chemistry. Ashwalkers have documented specific plant uses across generations without fully understanding why the compounds are present.

**The Drowned Coast:** The submerged city structures have been colonized by marine plant analogs — co-evolved with Nera fauna over eight centuries. Dense growth on submerged building surfaces, on the seafloor in shallower zones, around Shellmass colonies. From underwater, the submerged Drowned Coast looks more like a reef than a ruin.
