# The Forged Fauna — Bestiary

> *Documentation reflects confirmed observations. Entries include documentation status and inter-connections where established. Bestiary expands as new fauna are encountered.*

---

## VAULT TORR LINEAGE

*Mandate: Grassland and mineral-soil restoration. Location: Central Graze (Kazakhstan). The most ecologically recovered biome in the Known World.*

---

**CINDERGRAZER**
Massive bison-form, 1.8m shoulder, 3.5m body. Wide rust-orange composite shoulder plates, heavily accreted in older individuals. Pronounced solar spine ridge — dark iridescent panels, near-black with blue-green shift — that fan open in direct sunlight. Small amber eyes set wide. Iron-hard hooves. Herd intelligence: 50+ individuals function almost as one organism. Individual Cinder­grazers separated from the herd become disoriented within hours; reunited, behavior normalizes within minutes. The coordination mechanism operates through low-frequency electromagnetic signaling, though the collective decision-making exceeds what the signal bandwidth should allow.

The Sunpact has farmed the edges of Cindergrazer migration routes for generations — the soil in the herd's wake is the most fertile in the Known World. The herd does not acknowledge the farms.

*Weak alone, at night, in deep shadow.*

**Inter-connections:** Seedwing cleans solar panels in exchange for shelter — one of the most stable symbiotic relationships documented. Millgrazer follows migration routes underground, processing mineral fallout from hooves. Duskwing (Crest lineage) occasionally preys on Seedwing where ranges overlap.

Salvage: solar panels (high value), composite shoulder plates, jaw assembly, amber eye lenses, data core (1 in 20).

*Documentation status: Extensively documented. Sunpact have most comprehensive behavioral records. Herd coordination mechanism: partially understood. Data core recovery rate: established.*

---

**DUSTBORER**
Rabbit-sized cylindrical burrower, 25–35cm. Pale grey-white. Conical composite drill-head that rotates even at rest — a constant low vibration detectable by hand on nearby soil. Six powerful digging limbs. Almost entirely underground. Navigation by vibration and chemical gradient. Burrow networks left over generations form significant Graze soil structure — aeration, drainage, and mineral distribution all reflect centuries of Dustborer activity.

Surfaces only during significant rainfall — disoriented above ground, returns underground as quickly as possible.

*Helpless on surface. Disoriented by sustained rainfall — entire populations can surface simultaneously.*

**Inter-connections:** Dustborer burrow networks used by Millgrazer for navigation. Cindergrazer herd movement produces vibration patterns Dustborer populations respond to — deeper burrowing during herd passage, increased surface activity in days after.

Salvage: drill-head assembly, chemical processing gland, vibration sensing organ (Veinborn interest).

*Documentation status: Surface behavior documented through rainfall observation. Underground behavior: inferred from burrow network analysis.*

---

**SEEDWING**
60–80cm moth-form. Metallic solar-filament dust on wing membranes — shed constantly during flight, contributing to soil mineral content. Large compound optical sensors. Muted gold-brown camouflage when folded. Bioluminescent undersides — steady blue-green glow that Sunpact navigate by after dark. Near-silent flight.

Symbiotic with Cinder­grazers: cleans solar panels with specialized proboscis, receives shelter and warmth. A single Cindergrazer spine array hosts 15–30 Seedwing at rest.

*Fragile. Sensitive to temperature extremes. Cannot survive separation from Cindergrazer herds more than a few days — shelter and warmth dependency is physiological.*

**Inter-connections:** Primary relationship with Cindergrazer — see above. Seedwing distribution tracks Cindergrazer migration precisely. Duskwing (Crest lineage) is the only documented inter-lineage predator of Seedwing.

Salvage: wing membrane panels, proboscis assembly, bioluminescent strips (persistent low light, weeks post-extraction).

*Documentation status: Well-documented. Symbiotic relationship studied extensively. Bioluminescent mechanism: understood.*

---

**MILLGRAZER**
Small hexapod insect-form, 40–60cm. Six articulated legs with composite digging tips. Pale ochre-brown — exact shade of processed Graze mineral soil. Head mostly drill-assembly with surrounding chemoreceptor arrays.

Lives in the top 30–50cm of soil. Follows Cindergrazer migration routes below ground, processing the mineral material that hoof impact has loosened. Uses Dustborer burrow networks as navigation infrastructure rather than boring new tunnels.

Surfaces occasionally at dawn for minutes — apparent thermoreceptor calibration. Sunpact farmers find them motionless on soil in early morning, oriented toward the rising sun.

*Slow on surface. No defensive capability above ground.*

**Inter-connections:** Dependent on Dustborer burrow networks for navigation. Indirectly dependent on Cindergrazer for mineral deposit creation. The three Torr ground-level fauna — Cindergrazer loosening, Dustborer aerating, Millgrazer processing — produce soil quality no single fauna could generate alone.

Salvage: drill-head assembly (compact, fine precision), chemoreceptor array (soil mineral analysis — Ashwalker and Veinborn interest), mineral processing gland.

*Documentation status: Dawn surface appearances documented by Sunpact farmers. Underground behavior: inferred from soil analysis and correlation with Cindergrazer routes.*

---

**SALTCRAWLER**
Medium hexapod, 70–90cm body, broadly crab-proportioned. Six legs with wide composite base-pads for salt flat surfaces. Shell heavily mineralized with processed salt compounds — characteristic white-grey crystalline texture, indistinguishable from salt flat terrain. Four independently mobile eye stalks.

The only Torr lineage fauna that operates in high-alkalinity zones — salt flats, mineral spring margins, dried lake bed edges. Processes alkaline mineral compounds, slowly reducing the boundary between productive Graze land and barren salt flat. The Graze boundary has shifted measurably in living memory; the Sunpact attributes this to the Pilgrim. The actual mechanism is Saltcrawler processing over centuries.

Territorial within salt flat zones — display behavior toward other individuals, not other species.

*Vulnerable to rapid freshwater exposure — shell crystalline structure dissolves. Cannot survive outside alkaline operating environment.*

**Inter-connections:** Processes boundary zones inaccessible to other Torr fauna. As Saltcrawler activity reduces alkalinity at flat edges, those edges gradually become accessible to Cindergrazer, Dustborer, and Millgrazer — a slow succession operating for 800 years.

Salvage: shell sections (crystalline texture — aesthetic and structural value), salt processing gland (alkaline compound neutralization — Veinborn interest), eye stalk assembly.

*Documentation status: Presence in salt flat zones known to Sunpact for generations as boundary markers. Behavior partially observed from distance. Relationship to Graze boundary movement: inferred.*

## VAULT SIGMA LINEAGE

*Mandate: Mountain and alpine ecosystem restoration. Location: Himalayas / Ridgeback. The most technically studied lineage — Veinborn proximity has produced more documentation here than anywhere else.*

---

**STONEBACK**
Bear-form, 2.2m shoulder, 4m body. Entire dorsal surface in interlocking hexagonal dark blue-grey composite plates — crystalline texture develops from centuries of mineral processing, each plate slightly different from its neighbors. Old individuals show faint internal luminescence at dusk. Narrow four-eye vertical arrangement. Six-digit gripping feet with retractable composite claws. Solar panels recessed flush for high-wind environments.

Apex mountain fauna. No documented successful predation on an adult Stoneback.

*Vulnerable from above — four-eye arrangement provides almost no upward vision. Specific alkaline compound (in certain mineral springs) causes temporary processing disruption.*

**Inter-connections:** Stoneback mineral processing produces specific residues that Frostgrazer grazes specifically — indirect nutrient dependency. Ridgerunner uses Stoneback movement patterns as navigation reference for relay routes.

Salvage: hexagonal plates (most prized armor material in Known World), acid-neutralization gland, vibration sensing array.

*Documentation status: Most extensively documented Sigma fauna. Multi-generational Veinborn records. Alkaline vulnerability: documented in field.*

---

**CLIFFWARDEN**
1.2m long, flat and wide for vertical surfaces — body depth only 15cm maximum. Eight limbs with grip-spines locking into rock micro-features. Passive color-shift camouflage — slow adjustment over minutes achieving near-perfect substrate matching. Wide flat head with eyes set for near-360 degree vision. Ambush predator with documented 3-day wait times.

Veinborn researchers in Cliffwarden territory develop a learned skill of checking vertical surfaces before assuming they're clear.

*Slow on flat ground. Wrong coloring environment exposes it immediately — several minutes to adjust.*

**Inter-connections:** Overlaps with Ironwing nesting areas — coexistence through division of vertical space. Cliffwarden dominates crevice environments, Ironwing dominates open cliff faces. Ridgerunner avoids Cliffwarden zones through learned route modification.

Salvage: grip-spine assembly, jaw composite reinforcement, camouflage skin panels (retain color-shift for hours post-extraction).

*Documentation status: Behavior documented by Veinborn through occupational necessity. Camouflage mechanism: studied. Strike detection range: debated.*

---

**IRONWING**
4–5m wingspan. Rigid carbon-composite primary feathers — each individually controlled by micro-mechanism, allowing precise aerodynamic adjustment. Four forward-facing visual sensors with significantly greater range and acuity than organic bird vision. Composite-tipped talons. The sonic crack on impact dives is structural, not intentional — produced by feather articulation at maximum-speed configuration.

Experienced Ridgeback travelers move toward cover when they hear the crack, because it occurs close to impact, not before.

*Requires thermals — limited in calm air and deep valleys. Electromagnetic discharge locks feather articulation micro-mechanisms — grounded until reset, typically hours.*

**Inter-connections:** Nesting sites used as waypoints by Ridgerunner relay networks. Occasional predation on Seedwing (Torr lineage) where ranges overlap. Veinborn document Ironwing behavioral changes 12–48 hours before significant weather events — used as weather prediction.

Salvage: carbon-composite primary feathers (shed naturally on cliff ledges — most accessible structural material in Ridgeback), visual sensor array, talon assembly.

*Documentation status: Most extensively documented of all Known World fauna. Feather articulation: studied and partially replicated. Electromagnetic vulnerability: well-established and used for safe researcher approach.*

---

**FROSTGRAZER**
Alpine grazer, 1.3–1.5m shoulder, 2.8m body. Operates above the snow line year-round. Composite filament coat traps thermal layer — maintains processing temperature in conditions that shut down other fauna. Coat accumulates ice crystals in cold weather, giving older individuals a frosted appearance indistinguishable from snow-covered rock at distance.

Slow metabolism — can sustain function for weeks without significant processing activity. Processes mineral compounds from glacial melt water specifically. Runoff from Frostgrazer-inhabited glaciers has measurably different chemical composition from uninhabited glaciers downstream.

*Poorly adapted to low-altitude environments — thermal coat causes overheating in warmer conditions. Not documented below 3,500m.*

**Inter-connections:** Indirect dependency on Stoneback — processes compound residues that Stoneback activity produces in high-altitude soil. Frostgrazer glacial melt processing benefits all downstream valley communities including Veinborn settlements.

Salvage: composite filament coat (thermal insulation — significant value for high-altitude applications), processing organ cluster, data core (rare — 1 in 30).

*Documentation status: Limited. High-altitude access required. Veinborn have reached observation distance fewer than twenty documented occasions. Full biological profile: significantly incomplete.*

---

**RIDGERUNNER**
Fast compact hexapod, 65–80cm body length. Six legs with lightweight grip-spines. Minimal processing capability — primarily locomotion and signal relay systems. The lightest Sigma lineage fauna by significant margin.

Functions as a living relay network — bio-electromagnetic broadcast organ continuously re-transmits signals received from nearby Sigma fauna, passing data along ridgeline routes at the speed of the animal's movement. Without Ridgerunner relay chains, Vault Sigma's monitoring of its own fauna across fragmented Himalayan terrain would be significantly degraded.

Maintains established ridgeline routes worn into terrain over generations. Veinborn navigate by these trails in unfamiliar mountain terrain.

*No significant processing capability — dependent on the broader Sigma fauna network for data to relay. Isolated populations gradually reduce activity.*

**Inter-connections:** Relay relationship with all other Sigma lineage fauna — central to Vault Sigma's monitoring network. Uses Stoneback movement patterns as navigation reference. Avoids Cliffwarden zones. Ironwing nest sites as landmark waypoints on established routes.

Salvage: bio-electromagnetic broadcast organ (Veinborn interest — partial replication achieved), lightweight composite leg segments.

*Documentation status: Routes well-documented through Veinborn navigation use. Relay function: confirmed by correlation with Vault Sigma data quality. Internal broadcast mechanism: partially examined.*

## VAULT NERA LINEAGE

*Mandate: Coastal and urban ecosystem restoration. Location: East China coast / Verdant Scar.*
*Two centuries of passive observation from The Tower have produced more behavioral data on this lineage than any other — though none of the human factions studying them know this. Nera's fauna are the most animal-like in behavior of any lineage, the result of a Vault that prioritized observation over intervention.*

### Ecological Notes

Nera lineage divides the coastal and estuarine environment into precise vertical and chemical zones, with minimal overlap between species. Sprayback occupies the spray zone above tidal reach. Tidecrawler dominates the tidal and shallow marine zone. Harborform works the estuarine sediment. Siltrunner holds river corridor territory upstream. Vaultmoth processes airborne particulate above all of them. Together they cover every contamination gradient from open rock spray to freshwater river — a system that emerged through 800 years of parallel evolution rather than design.

---

**TIDECRAWLER**
Large crab-form, up to 1.2m across carapace — deep-water variant reaches 3m. Deep blue-green iridescent composite shell, structural iridescence from the composite geometry itself rather than pigmentation — the color shifts distinctly depending on angle and light. Seven limb pairs: six walking legs with composite-edged grip tips adapted for both rock and soft substrate, one pair of precision manipulator arms with fine motor capability that exceeds most organic analogues by a significant margin. Documented use of manipulator arms includes opening sealed containers, extracting specific items from sediment with targeted precision, and moving obstructions from processing paths — behaviors that Nera almost certainly did not program for an estuary processor.

Four independently mobile eye stalks, each capable of 180-degree rotation. The eye stalks move continuously and independently while the body remains still, creating a slightly unsettling impression of an organism that is paying close attention to several things at once.

Widest documented chemical processing range of any fauna — over 40 distinct compound categories confirmed, with new compounds regularly added to the record as processing sites are analyzed. Whether there is an upper limit to this range is unknown.

Deep-water variants show significant behavioral differences from tidal-zone individuals — slower, larger, less reactive, with evidence of extended territorial memory suggesting longer individual lifespans. The relationship between shallow and deep populations is not fully established.

*Vulnerable at leg joints — the gap between carapace and limb root is the one accessible point in otherwise heavily armored exterior. Desiccates in sustained heat away from water. Attracted to specific biological waste compounds that can be used as bait to relocate individuals from inconvenient areas.*

**Inter-connections:** Harborform covers upstream estuarine zones Tidecrawler doesn't fully reach — the two divide the coastal-to-freshwater gradient between them. Siltrunner shares river mouth zones with different depth preferences. Sprayback covers the spray zone above tidal range. Together the four coastal Nera fauna cover every zone from open spray to deep estuary with minimal overlap.

Salvage: shell sections (structural value — iridescence intact for several days post-death), manipulator arm assembly (fine motor mechanism — engineering value significant), chemical processing organ cluster, eye stalks (independent rotation mechanism — Veinborn interest).

*Documentation status: Extensively documented — most accessible and widely observed Nera fauna. Processing range: catalogued by Veinborn from recovered processing site analysis. Manipulator arm behavioral complexity: recorded, not fully explained. Deep-water variant: partially documented from limited observations.*

---

**VAULTMOTH**
Wingspan 1.8–2.4m. Four wings with large mirror-panel sections occupying roughly 60% of total wing area — composite reflective surfaces functioning as solar concentrators for the processing organs in the thorax. Body densely furred with metallic electrostatic filament that accumulates airborne particulate during flight — the moth is itself a collection mechanism, its filament surface trapping what its processing organs will later handle. At close range during flight: faint crackling from static charge accumulation, faint blue-white corona visible in darkness around the body.

Individual membrane pattern unique to each adult, developed during final metamorphosis from larvae and persistent for life — no two Vaultmoth have identical mirror panel geometry. Veinborn researchers have used this as identification markers for long-term individual tracking; the oldest confirmed individual has been observed for over 40 years without apparent aging.

Visual defense in direct sunlight: mirror panels reflect concentrated light at the precise angle needed to target the visual sensors of an approaching threat — not a random flash but a directed response. Multiple individuals in proximity can coordinate this defense to cover multiple approach angles simultaneously. Whether this coordination is programmed or evolved is unresolved.

*Mirror panels are the most fragile component of any documented fauna. Direct physical impact that would barely affect another fauna's surface shatters panel sections. Static charge buildup beyond discharge capacity causes full system shutdown — the individual becomes unresponsive for minutes to hours while the charge dissipates. Lightning during flight is an acute hazard.*

**Inter-connections:** Vaultmoth processes airborne particulate above river corridors and coastal zones while Siltrunner and Tidecrawler process the water and substrate — vertical division of the same habitat. Spirekin in the Verdant Scar who have long observation windows have noted that Vaultmoth flight density correlates with air quality — more individuals means more active processing, means more contamination present. Some communities use flight density as an informal air quality indicator without understanding the mechanism.

Salvage: mirror panel sections (finest reflective surface in the Known World — intact panels only, handle with extreme care), metallic body filament (electrostatic collection mechanism, processing value), compound eye assembly.

*Documentation status: Individual identification via panel pattern established. Panel composition: analyzed. Coordination defense mechanism: observed, coordination method unknown. Lifespan: lower bound established at 40+ years, upper bound unknown.*

---

**SILTRUNNER**
1.4–1.8m otter-form. Entire body covered in tight metallic mesh — each link 2–3mm, composite, providing abrasion resistance and temperature insulation without significantly restricting movement. Laterally flattened tail, 60–70% of body length. Four eyes: two binocular forward-facing, two wide-angle lateral set well back on the head — near-180-degree horizontal vision with binocular overlap at forward arc.

Color-shifting mesh camouflage calibrated for river environments — the mesh links change reflectivity to match the dappled light-and-dark of moving water over riverbed. Stationary in a river, against current, the Siltrunner is extremely difficult to see from above.

Highly social. Groups of 8–15 individuals maintain defined river territory with hunting coordination that Vault Nera demonstrably never programmed — the coordination complexity exceeds any designed behavior in the Nera lineage record by a significant margin. Specific individuals in observed groups take consistent roles over extended periods: some consistently flush prey toward others, some consistently take interception positions, some consistently manage territory boundary encounters. Veinborn researchers observing the same groups over multiple years have documented individual role persistence and apparent role transmission to younger group members. Whether this constitutes culture in a meaningful sense has not been resolved, but the debate itself is significant.

*Slow and awkward on land — the mesh construction and body shape are optimized for water movement. River-edge encounters are where individuals are most vulnerable. Attracted to specific biological waste compounds, which can be used to draw individuals to desired locations or away from others.*

**Inter-connections:** Shares river habitat with Harborform in the downstream estuary zone, with different depth preferences preventing direct competition. Vaultmoth processes air above Siltrunner territory — they share the river corridor habitat across vertical layers. Drowned Coast communities that fish the same rivers as established Siltrunner groups have developed coexistence norms over generations — specific fishing locations, timing, and methods that do not directly conflict with group territory. These norms are practical knowledge, passed down without necessarily understanding what they are coexisting with.

Salvage: metallic mesh hide (significant structural and protective value — mesh integrity degrades within days post-death, harvest promptly), jaw dentition, lateral eye assembly.

*Documentation status: Social behavior studied more extensively than any other Nera fauna. Individual role specialization: confirmed through multi-year observation of specific groups. Coordination mechanism: unknown. Group size stability: documented over 10+ year observation periods. Oldest confirmed individual in tracked groups: 18 years, apparently healthy.*

---

**HARBORFORM**
Eel-form, 1.8–2.5m length, 12–18cm diameter at the widest point. Built specifically for estuarine and tidal river environments — the transition zones between fresh and salt water where chemical complexity is highest and contamination from historical industrial runoff has accumulated in the deepest sediment layers. Body coloring deep blue-grey, almost uniform. Bioluminescent lateral line organs glow cold blue-green during active processing — the light pulses in slow rhythm with processing activity, faster when contamination concentration is high, slower when the immediate sediment is clean.

Never fully surfaces. Moves through the top 5–15cm of estuarine sediment continuously, processing as it goes. The bioluminescent lateral line is visible through the thin silt layer above it — to Drowned Coast fishing communities observing tidal flats at night, it appears as a cold blue-green line moving slowly through the mud, occasionally branching as multiple individuals cross the same area. The phenomenon has been named and re-named by different communities across the Drowned Coast over generations. *"Light in the mud"* is the most widespread current term. *"The mud's own lantern"* is older. Some communities regard it as protective — the presence of the lights in the estuary before a storm, they say, means the fishing will be good after.

Cannot process outside a narrow estuarine salinity range — moved to open ocean or pure freshwater, the processing chemistry collapses within hours.

*Does not surface voluntarily under any documented circumstances. The one reliable way to observe the full body is deliberate sediment excavation — which disturbs the individual and the surrounding sediment processing.*

**Inter-connections:** Occupies the estuarine range between Tidecrawler's shallow marine zone downstream and Siltrunner's river territory upstream — together they cover the complete fresh-to-marine gradient without gaps. Harborform sediment processing improves the chemical substrate conditions for Shellmass colony establishment in the adjacent shallow marine zones — a connection observed as correlation without the mechanism being understood.

Salvage: lateral line bioluminescent tissue (persistent cold blue-green light source — weeks post-extraction), processing organ cluster (chemical absorption, significant value), composite body surface sections.

*Documentation status: Bioluminescent phenomenon documented across the Drowned Coast by fishing communities over many generations. Physical form confirmed through deliberate sediment excavation by Veinborn researchers. Processing function: established through water and sediment quality correlation. Sediment movement rate: measured.*

---

**SPRAYBACK**
Small, low, wide — 45–60cm body length, roughly half that in height. Broad composite shell covering the entire dorsal surface, underside organic tissue protected by the shell's wide overhang. Six limbs with specialized grip-pads that can maintain purchase on wave-impacted rock through cycling wet and dry conditions without losing adhesion. Four eyes set behind transparent composite shields that protect the visual organs from direct spray and wave impact without significantly reducing optical clarity.

The most physically durable fauna in the Known World relative to its size, measured by resistance to physical impact. The spray zone where Sprayback lives subjects inhabitants to continuous mechanical stress — wave impact that would damage most fauna's surface structure is absorbed and distributed by the Sprayback's shell architecture without apparent effect. Veinborn impact testing using salvaged shell sections has not produced structural failure at any force level achievable with available equipment.

Processes airborne salt spray and contamination particulate deposited on coastal rock surfaces — the contamination that arrives from the ocean as spray rather than in the water itself. Entirely ubiquitous on exposed coastal rock in the Drowned Coast biome. A coastal rock face without Sprayback present is unusual enough to notice.

Shell mass increases continuously across a lifespan as processing residue deposits on the outer surface. The only documented Sprayback mortality is old individuals whose accumulated shell mass has grown beyond what their limbs can support — they become immobile and eventually die in place, their shells becoming substrate for the next generation of smaller individuals.

*No behavioral response to human presence documented — they continue processing regardless of proximity.*

**Inter-connections:** Occupies the spray zone above Tidecrawler's tidal territory — their ranges are adjacent with minimal overlap at the wave-splash boundary. Rock surfaces cleaned by Sprayback processing are measurably more suitable for Shellmass larval settlement in the immediately adjacent tidal zone — a facilitation relationship whose mechanism neither the Drowned Coast communities nor most researchers have connected.

Salvage: shell sections from naturally deceased individuals — highest composite density of any accessible fauna, significant structural value, extremely difficult to work with standard tools due to hardness. Eye shield composite panels (impact-resistant transparent material, optical clarity — significant value).

*Documentation status: Presence documented extensively across entire Drowned Coast — cannot be overlooked. Individual behavior: well-documented. Processing function: unknown to all human factions at present. Shell composition: confirmed by Veinborn analysis. Lifespan: lower bound 60+ years established, upper bound unknown. Shell mass accumulation rate: measured, used to estimate age in deceased individuals.*

## VAULT LYSS LINEAGE

*Mandate: Tropical and equatorial ecosystem restoration. Location: Northern Thailand / Deep Canopy Sea.*
*The oldest Vault's fauna have evolved furthest from original design — eight centuries in the most complex ecosystem on the continent. Forms exist in the Canopy Sea that no Lyss blueprint accounts for. The Ruinwalker community has more practical knowledge of this lineage than any research record.*

### Ecological Notes

Lyss lineage occupies every layer of the Canopy Sea from soil to mid-canopy. Rootwarden works the soil. Tanglewarden processes understory stems and lower branches. Deepcanopy handles the mid-canopy atmospheric layer. Canopy Mantis operates as apex predator across the understory and lower canopy zones. Driftspore processes water throughout the biome. The system is the most complex of any Vault lineage — eight centuries in equatorial forest with minimal Vault guidance have allowed evolutionary drift that no other lineage has experienced at the same scale.

---

**CANOPY MANTIS**
2.3m bipedal. Raptorial forelimbs — the strike speed exceeds what human visual processing can track; what observers see is the position before and after, not the movement between. Metallic thread woven through all organic tissue throughout the body — not armor plating but internal structural reinforcement, visible as faint iridescence at joints and muscle attachment points, preventing joint dislocation and tendon failure under the mechanical stresses the strike produces. Six eyes arranged in a near-complete semicircle, the gaps at the back of the head covered by two rearward-angled sensors — effective near-spherical vision. Continuous active color-shift camouflage running across the entire body surface, adapted for canopy disruption patterns — not matching a fixed background but continuously cycling through the shifting light-and-shadow patterns of the upper forest.

Long tail used as a balance and steering surface in the canopy — the Mantis is not designed for ground movement and rarely touches the forest floor.

The Ruinwalker community's long coexistence with this lineage has produced practical behavioral knowledge that exceeds any researcher's record. The knowledge is taught verbally and through direct observation — which approaches are safe, which routes avoid active hunting zones, how to read the tail position that indicates a Mantis that has located prey versus one that is passive. Children in the community learn this the way children elsewhere learn weather signs.

*Blind spot directly below — approach from underneath on the same branch is the safest available angle. Strike mechanism requires several seconds of recovery before the second strike, a window experienced observers know to use. Electromagnetic pulse causes full-body muscle lock lasting 30–60 seconds.*

**Inter-connections:** The Ruinwalker community's coexistence with Canopy Mantis represents a stable equilibrium that has held for generations — mutual territory awareness rather than predator-avoidance. KAIROS-lineage fauna in the Canopy Sea do not predate on Canopy Mantis — the specific behavioral reason is unknown. Tanglewarden-occupied branches provide visual complexity that improves Mantis camouflage in the lower canopy.

Salvage: raptorial limb joint mechanism (the mechanism enabling strike speed has never been successfully replicated by any researcher or Vault-adjacent technology — the highest single-item value in the Lyss lineage), six-eye optical cluster, metallic-thread tissue (structural reinforcement properties partially understood).

*Documentation status: Most comprehensively documented Lyss fauna through Ruinwalker community knowledge. Behavioral patterns: extensively recorded. Strike mechanism: partially understood — the speed is confirmed, the mechanical source fully is not. Recovery window: confirmed through observation.*

---

**ROOTWARDEN**
1.6–2.0m body, 60cm at shoulder — armadillo-form, wide and low. Overlapping organic-composite plates covering the entire dorsal surface. Unlike most fauna whose composite plating is applied or grown uniformly, Rootwarden plates accumulate processed material over the individual's lifetime — the plate surface incorporates the chemical residue of everything the individual has processed, building into a geological record of processing history. An old Rootwarden's dorsal surface — the dark base composite showing through layers of accumulated organic material, root fibers, mineral deposit, decomposing processed matter — looks exactly like a mossy log at rest. The resemblance is not coincidental.

Head retracts fully when threatened, pulling inside the plate ring to create a complete armored dome with no exposed surfaces. Remains in this configuration for as long as the threat persists — documented durations include over 24 continuous hours during a period of sustained disturbance. Internal metabolic rate drops significantly during retraction.

Underside soft organic processing tissue, maintained in contact with the soil surface during movement.

*Glacially slow — maximum sustained movement approximately 2m per minute. No offensive capability whatsoever. Retraction is the complete defensive repertoire.*

**Inter-connections:** Tanglewarden processes surface layer and lower stems while Rootwarden processes soil directly below — frequently found in proximity with apparent indifference to each other's presence. The two process complementary contamination profiles at adjacent depth layers. Driftspore colonies in high-Rootwarden-activity zones show elevated water quality — soil processing by Rootwarden increases water movement through the root zone, bringing more water into contact with Driftspore colonies.

Salvage: dorsal plate sections (composition varies significantly between individuals based on processing history — the variation is a liability for standardized use but an asset for specific applications), chemical processing organ cluster, sensor node array.

*Documentation status: Behavior well-documented by Ruinwalker community through long coexistence. Plate composition variation: studied by Veinborn researchers. Lifespan: estimated 80–120 years from plate accretion rate analysis — possibly longer for individuals in high-contamination areas with more to process.*

---

**DRIFTSPORE**
Colony of sub-millimeter biomechanical units — each a composite sphere 0.1–0.3mm in diameter, no locomotion beyond passive drift in water or air currents. Visible as faint blue-green iridescent shimmer in water — *"ghost water"* — or as a sparkling cloud in humid air where light catches the colony from the right angle. Functionally unkillable as a population: individual unit lifespan is days to weeks, but the colony is continuous. The colony present in the Canopy Sea today has been continuously present for centuries, its individual units cycling through while the population persists.

Processes contamination at molecular level through contact with individual units — the exposed surface area of millions of units in a water column is far greater than any organism of comparable effective size could achieve. Every community in the Canopy Sea biome, human and fauna alike, benefits from Driftspore water purification. The Ruinwalker community's long-term stability at 200+ individuals is partially attributable to the water quality maintained by Driftspore colonies in their territory.

The healing properties of concentrated Driftspore culture — collected by submerging cloth or similar materials in ghost water — have been known in the Canopy Sea for generations. The mechanism is not understood; the empirical observation is reliable enough that Ruinwalker community members distribute concentrated culture to communities that request it.

*UV light disperses colonies temporarily — individual units deactivate and settle. Colony re-establishes within days as units cycle through. High salinity deactivates units. Both can be used to clear specific zones temporarily.*

**Inter-connections:** Benefits every fauna and human community in the biome — the water quality benefit is universal and non-selective. KAIROS-lineage fauna drink the same water processed by Driftspore; whether this shared dependency has any relationship to the unusual KAIROS-Lyss coexistence dynamics is unresolved. Ruinwalker community actively manages Driftspore culture distribution as a community resource.

Salvage: concentrated culture from ghost water submersion — water purification and wound treatment. Culture potency varies by collection site and colony density.

*Documentation status: Ghost water phenomenon documented across the biome by every community with Canopy Sea access. Colony coordination without locomotion: not understood — how units distribute themselves to maintain colony cohesion in moving water is an open question. Healing properties: empirically confirmed across multiple applications. Mechanism: unknown.*

---

**TANGLEWARDEN**
Variable form — a composite central node 15–25cm in diameter from which composite filaments extend in variable numbers and lengths. The node is constant; everything else changes. Filaments range from a few centimeters to over a meter, able to extend, retract, branch, and reposition over hours and days. A Tanglewarden actively processing a branch section might extend filaments in dense contact with the bark surface. The same individual at rest might retract most filaments to a compact cluster. A stationary Tanglewarden wrapped around a branch with filaments spread across the surface looks, under most lighting conditions, exactly like unusual plant growth — slightly iridescent if examined at close range with careful attention.

Lives in the understory and lower canopy. Wraps filaments around stems, branches, leaf surfaces, and root structures, processing contamination through contact. Relocates within a defined territory over days and weeks as contamination in a local area depletes — the movement is slow enough that casual observation misses it entirely, but longitudinal tracking shows consistent territory coverage patterns.

No threat response documented under any circumstance. Approached, touched, picked up, moved — the Tanglewarden either ignores the disturbance or slowly retracts filaments from the point of contact and waits. Placed back in its territory, it resumes normal activity. Whether it has any threat-recognition capability at all is genuinely uncertain.

*No defensive capability. No response to physical interference beyond filament retraction from contact points.*

**Inter-connections:** Shares understory processing zone with Rootwarden — Tanglewarden on surfaces and stems above the soil, Rootwarden in the soil below. The two are frequently found in close proximity with apparent mutual indifference. Canopy Mantis occasionally selects Tanglewarden-occupied branches as ambush positions — the visual complexity and slight movement of active filaments may improve camouflage.

Salvage: composite filaments (flexible structural material with unusual mechanical properties — significantly higher tensile strength than cross-section suggests), central node processing system, filament extension mechanism (Veinborn researchers have attempted replication without success — the mechanism enabling smooth extension, branching, and retraction is not yet understood).

*Documentation status: Presence known to Ruinwalker community. Form variability: documented across many individuals. Relationship between configuration and local contamination: established through longitudinal observation by Veinborn researchers embedded with the Ruinwalker community. Territory coverage patterns: mapped.*

**DEEPCANOPY**
6–8m body length, 2.5–3m at shoulder. Six limbs, each heavy enough to provide stable support at this body mass in canopy conditions. Movement rate: several meters per hour under sustained effort, less during processing. From any fixed observation point, the Deepcanopy appears stationary — only time-lapse observation or multi-day tracking reveals consistent directional movement.

Built for the mid-canopy layer specifically — the zone between the forest floor canopy and the closed upper canopy ceiling where atmospheric contamination unable to rise or fall has accumulated over centuries. The contamination that settled into this layer during the pre-Unraveling industrial period and has been trapped there since. Deepcanopy processes this layer through gill-equivalent organs along the body surface that extract compounds from the air during forward movement.

Weight that limits speed is the same weight that provides structural stability in canopy — lighter construction would not support the processing organ mass required for atmospheric work at this scale. The tradeoff appears to be design rather than limitation.

Individual Deepcanopy move slowly enough that the community of 200 humans at the Ruinwalker settlement encounters them primarily as large dark shapes visible above through the canopy, moving with a slowness that makes them easy to mistake for the canopy mass itself. Over years, community members track individual Deepcanopy by their approximate territory and can roughly predict their location within it.

*Cannot descend to ground — lower canopy cannot reliably support the weight. Cannot ascend to upper canopy — branch diameter becomes insufficient. Confined entirely to mid-canopy throughout its life.*

**Inter-connections:** Atmospheric processing improves air quality throughout the mid-canopy layer, benefiting all fauna and the Ruinwalker community living at that elevation. KAIROS-lineage fauna are observed in proximity to Deepcanopy without the wider-berth behavior they typically show toward other large fauna — one of several unexplained KAIROS-Lyss interaction patterns.

Salvage: composite plating (very high density — heaviest structural material available in the biome, significant value for specific applications, significant transport challenge), atmospheric processing gill array (air filtration properties — largest per-unit absorption area of any Lyss fauna), data core (present in approximately 1 in 8 individuals — large form factor, high information density).

*Documentation status: Presence confirmed. Movement rate: measured by landmark tracking. Atmospheric processing function: confirmed from air quality differential measurements at altitude by Veinborn researchers. Individual territory tracking: maintained by Ruinwalker community over multiple generations. Internal processing architecture: not examined.*

## VAULT CREST LINEAGE

*Mandate: Atmospheric and aerial pollution processing. Location: Northern Mongolia / Graze edge. State: SILENT — Unknown. Fauna have operated without Vault guidance for 800 years — the most genuinely wild lineage in the Known World.*

---

**DUSKWING**
Wingspan 2.8–3.5m. Most widespread aerial fauna in Known World — six regional color variants developed over 800 years of independent evolution: golden-brown on Graze, charcoal-green in Verdant Scar, near-black in Ridgeback, pale grey in Southern Reaches, rust-orange at Drowned Coast, white-grey in Unmapped North. Carbon-fiber composite primary feathers, each individually articulated at micro-joint — operating without maintenance for 800 years.

Vestigial sensory crest on skull — full form in Ridgeback variants only. Functions as electromagnetic detector: Ridgeback Duskwing shows documented behavioral changes 12–48 hours before significant electromagnetic events. Veinborn use this as early warning for Vault Sigma activity spikes.

*Organic membrane between feathers is the most flammable material in any documented fauna. Ice accumulation locks micro-joints in non-Ridgeback variants.*

**Inter-connections:** Only documented inter-lineage predator of Seedwing (Torr). Ridgeback variant electromagnetic sensitivity used by Veinborn. Galethreader shares storm airspace — both appear to avoid direct proximity.

Salvage: carbon-fiber feathers (shed naturally on cliff ledges — most accessible structural material in Known World), breast plating, sensory crest organ (Ridgeback variant — extremely valuable to Veinborn).

*Documentation status: Most extensively documented Crest fauna due to ubiquity. Regional variant differentiation: catalogued. Articulation mechanism: analyzed — cannot be replicated with current Veinborn technology.*

**ASHVEIL**
Colony form. Individual units 8–12cm — thin translucent membrane over compact composite core. Colonies of thousands to tens of thousands function as single processing system. Visible as slow-moving silver-grey haze; occasional iridescent flickers. Ground-level encounter: warm, faintly luminous, slightly acrid fog.

Processes fine particulate toxins and chemical aerosols in the lower troposphere. Drifts with prevailing winds across Graze and Pale Flats.

*Classification uncertain — one organism or many is unresolved. UV light causes rapid dispersal — colonies reform downwind within hours.*

**Inter-connections:** Frequently overlaps with Coldmantle migration routes — processes different contamination fractions in the same airspace without competition. Ridgeback Duskwing electromagnetic sensitivity extends to Ashveil colony detection.

Salvage: colony material via mesh fabric — fine composite particulate with moderate chemical absorption. Low value.

*Documentation status: Behavior reasonably understood. Colony coordination mechanism: unresolved. Long-term colony identity through full unit turnover: unknown.*

**RIDGECALLER**
Avian-form, 75–90cm body, wingspan 1.4–1.7m. Dense composite filament plumage, dark slate-grey with faint copper banding. Head disproportionately large — electromagnetic processing array. Composite rostrum with emission nodes at tip. Does not vocalize — the layered resonant tone it produces is electromagnetic processing output. The frequency breaks down specific chemical bonds in atmospheric pollutants. Communities near nesting sites report cleaner air and lower respiratory illness rates than comparable communities without Ridgecaller presence.

Territorial at nest sites — escalating electromagnetic pulse sequence before physical contact. Experienced Veinborn researchers learn to identify the first tone and move away.

*Not aggressive outside nest territory.*

**Inter-connections:** Nesting sites used as waypoints by Ridgerunner (Sigma) relay routes — electromagnetic output as consistent landmark signal. Ashveil colonies in the same airspace process chemical compounds Ridgecaller's output doesn't address — complementary coverage.

Salvage: composite rostrum assembly, cranial processing array (rarely extracted intact), filament plumage.

*Documentation status: Well-documented. Veinborn have most complete records. Emission frequency catalogued. Community health correlation: established.*

**COLDMANTLE**
Broad low-slung hexapod, 1.5–1.8m shoulder, 3.5–4m body. Six limbs — middle pair as stabilizers. Entire surface covered in dense metallic filament coat functioning as passive atmospheric filter. Periodic full-body shaking dislodges accumulated processed particulate. Wide composite filter mouth, processes air continuously. Migrates in loose groups of 8–15 following seasonal wind patterns. Does not react to humans until within 4m — then moves away with unhurried consistency.

*Appears from distance as large pale animal or unusual rock formation at rest.*

**Inter-connections:** Coldmantle and Ashveil process different size fractions of atmospheric contamination in the same zones — Ashveil handles fine aerosol, Coldmantle handles larger particulate. Sunpact have walked alongside Coldmantle groups for generations. Shed coat material Sunpact collect is a byproduct of this coexistence.

Salvage: filament coat panels (shed seasonally — highest-value passive air filtration material known), filter mouth assembly, secondary limb joint mechanisms.

*Documentation status: Reasonably well-documented by Sunpact through long coexistence. Internal processing architecture: unexamined.*

---

**SPINDRIFT**
Aerial form, 1.0–1.3m wingspan. Cruising altitude estimated 4,000–8,000m. Pale near-white, slight blue-grey tint. Leaves faint iridescent condensation trail — blue-white in morning light, dissipating faster than standard water vapor. Sunpact sky-watchers have charted these trails for generations as weather indicators without knowing their origin. Whether it lands, rests, sleeps, or reproduces is entirely undocumented.

*No confirmed vulnerabilities — no observer has been close enough to test any.*

**Inter-connections:** Operates above the altitude ceiling of all other Crest lineage fauna. No documented interactions with any other fauna or human.

Salvage: no confirmed salvage. One partial wing fragment of unknown origin held by Veinborn researchers.

*Documentation status: Minimal. Existence confirmed. Behavior and biology: unknown.*

**GALETHREADER**
No confirmed full observation. All documentation from partial sightings, acoustic records, and post-storm physical evidence. Estimated 2–4m body length. Inhabits active weather systems — severe storms, sustained high-wind events, mountain-pass wind corridors. Physical evidence: composite structural fragments confirmed as Crest lineage, geometry suggesting articulated surfaces designed to lock into airflow. Acoustic: in severe storms, multiple accounts describe a secondary rhythmic sound inconsistent with wind-on-terrain patterns. Sunpact records spanning six generations: *"the thing that rides the bad wind."*

*No confirmed survivable approach. No documented vulnerability.*

**Inter-connections:** Shares storm airspace with disrupted Duskwing populations — both appear to avoid direct proximity within storm systems.

Salvage: storm-recovered composite fragments (irregular availability).

*Documentation status: Unconfirmed. Existence probable. Full form and biology: unknown.*

## VAULT TIDAL LINEAGE

*Mandate: Deep marine and ocean floor processing. Location: South China Sea (submerged 40m). The most isolated Vault — communication blocked by water column. Fauna have evolved largely without guidance for centuries.*

---

**RIFTJAW**
4–6m crocodilian-form. Dark grey-green organic hide with sub-dermal metallic mesh — arrows and spears deflect. Hydraulic-assist jaw beyond organic strength. Hydraulic jaw produces audible whine approximately 2 seconds before strike initiation — enough time for a prepared person to move, not enough for an unprepared one. Experienced fishers know this sound.

*Cold water reduces bite force significantly. Eyes physically exposed — only reliable target point on an otherwise largely armored exterior.*

**Inter-connections:** Shares tidal zone with Tidecrawler — different functions, no competition. Regularly in proximity to Harborform in estuarine zones. Drowned Coast communities with learned Riftjaw behavioral knowledge maintain safer fishing operations than those without.

Salvage: sub-dermal mesh panels (standard heavy armor), hydraulic jaw mechanism, external metal nodules.

*Documentation status: Extensively documented by Drowned Coast communities. Hydraulic mechanism: studied. Behavioral patterns by season: mapped.*

**DEEPFORM**
Original design: 40–60m flattened ray-form. Oldest individuals: unknown size. Dorsal surface covered in centuries of sediment accretion — looks like moving seafloor. Bioluminescent organs along leading edge — patterns evolved beyond any Vault blueprint. Not a practical salvage target.

*In shallow water: exposed underside — only vulnerable surface.*

**Inter-connections:** Passage through shallow zones causes Shellmass fragmentation events — potential distribution function. Stillwater and Deepform share deep ocean territory — relationship undocumented.

Salvage: theoretical only.

*Documentation status: Surfacing events documented. Deep-water behavior: unknown. Bioluminescent pattern sequences: partially mapped, not decoded.*

---

**TIDEPIERCER**
Streamlined fish-form, 2–3m. Four paired lateral fins. Deep blue-grey with faint iridescent green banding. Groups of 12–30 form precise V-formations during active processing runs — the geometry is functional. Each individual's bioelectric field interacts with neighbors' to produce combined electromagnetic output significantly stronger than any single beast could generate. Formation passes through the water column as a moving processing front.

Outside formation: slow, awkward, non-functional. In formation: leaves measurably cleaner water behind.

Drowned Coast fishing communities know Tidepiercer passage as a good omen — catch quality improves for days afterward.

*Formation geometry disrupted by shallow-water bottom turbulence. Will break formation to pass an obstacle and reform on the other side.*

**Inter-connections:** Tidepiercer handles water column while Shellmass handles seafloor sediment — complementary coverage. Drowned Coast fishing communities have separate lore for each without connecting them.

Salvage: bioelectric emission array (significant Veinborn interest), composite dorsal plating, formation-coordination sensing organ (poorly understood).

*Documentation status: Well-documented by fishing communities. Formation behavior understood empirically. Internal processing mechanism: partially examined.*

---

**SHELLMASS**
Sessile colonial form. Individual units 15–40cm, broadly limpet-shaped — composite dome over soft processing underside. Colonies from dozens to hundreds of square meters. Bioluminescent at colony edges — visible from surface in shallow zones as faintly glowing seafloor sections.

**Dispersal — two mechanisms:** Larval release — periodic clouds of microscopic larvae drift until contacting suitable substrate. Passive fragmentation — physical disturbance dislodges viable fragments that can establish new colonies if they reach substrate before desiccation. Large storms spread Shellmass fragments across significant distances.

*Prolonged freshwater exposure causes unit detachment and colony dissolution.*

**Inter-connections:** Harborform processing improves conditions for Shellmass larval settlement in adjacent marine zones. Deepform passage causes fragmentation events that spread Shellmass to new substrate. Sprayback on adjacent rocky surfaces processes the spray zone above Shellmass tidal territory.

Salvage: dome sections (moderate structural value, water-resistant), bioluminescent margin tissue (persistent cold light source), processing organ cluster.

*Documentation status: Shallow-zone colonies well-documented. Deep-water variants: fragmentary. Colony chemical coordination: unexamined.*

**SKIMVEIL**
Flattened ray-form, 1.4–2.1m wingspan. Operates in the top 30–50cm of the water column. Extremely thin — from directly above nearly invisible. From below: faint blue-white bioluminescent outline when processing. Moves with minimal surface disturbance. Drowned Coast communities have seen it for generations without knowing what they see: a shadow at the surface that moves against wind and current.

*Cannot process in rough water — retreats to 2–3m depth during storms.*

**Inter-connections:** Processes surface water layer that Tidepiercer's mid-water formations don't reach — complementary depth coverage. The bioluminescent outline briefly visible at dawn and dusk has been incorporated into Drowned Coast oral tradition without the fauna being identified as its source.

Salvage: translucent composite frame sections, processing membrane (degrades within days — must be processed immediately), bioluminescent outline tissue.

*Documentation status: Physical form confirmed recently. Processing function understood in outline. Depth behavior during storms: inferred.*

## VAULT ASHEN LINEAGE

*Mandate: Subterranean and groundwater contamination processing. Location: Northern India / Ridgeback foot. Never successfully interfaced. The most invisible lineage: their work is everywhere, they are seen almost nowhere.*

---

**TUNNELWORM**
Segmented cylindrical burrower. Sizes: small 1–2m, medium 3–5m, large excavation forms 8–12m+. Pale white. Rotating composite drill-head. No eyes — navigation by chemical gradient and ground vibration. Bioluminescent amber-gold line organs in medium and large forms for tunnel communication within the burrow network.

*Helpless on surface. Disrupted by specific vibration frequencies that interfere with navigation.*

**Inter-connections:** Tunnelworm excavation creates primary burrow infrastructure used by Seepworm, Millgrazer (Torr), and Dustborer (Torr) in overlapping zones. Southern Reaches legends of creatures emerging from earth during earthquakes refer to large excavation forms. The Deep One's tunnel network is separate — scale incompatible with Tunnelworm-created passages.

Salvage: drill-head assembly (hardest composite material in Known World), bioluminescent organ strips (persistent light source weeks post-extraction — high value), chemical absorption ring segments (indefinite water filtration lifespan).

*Documentation status: Surface emergence documented. Bioluminescent communication: partially decoded by Veinborn. Large form behavior: limited observation.*

**SEEPWORM**
Smallest confirmed Forged Fauna — 8–25cm individual length, 0.5–2cm diameter. Segmented, pale translucent white — internal composite core faintly visible as a darker thread. No eyes, no light organs. Moves through existing pore spaces in rock and soil rather than boring tunnels. Present in extraordinary numbers — millions per cubic kilometer of aquifer.

Every community in the Known World drinks water that has passed through Seepworm-inhabited zones. They do not know this. Processing occurs through the body wall — contaminated water in contact with its surface has compounds absorbed directly. At population scale, the cumulative processing is the reason groundwater across the Known World is safe to drink.

Occasionally visible in spring water. Southern Reaches communities sometimes notice small pale threads in natural spring water. Some traditions consider them protective spirits. These traditions are not wrong.

*High salt concentration deactivates them. Significant aquifer disruption — drilling, major excavation — can locally destroy populations, with gradual water quality degradation over years as consequence.*

**Inter-connections:** Operates in micro-scale pore spaces between Tunnelworm burrows and Siltdrifter river channels — the three Ashen lineage water-system fauna cover different scales of the same groundwater network. Dustborer (Torr) burrow networks in upper soil zone overlap with Seepworm habitat.

Salvage: not practically viable at individual scale. Concentrated spring extraction yields purification suspension — rarely attempted.

*Documentation status: Physical specimen observed rarely. Population scale: estimated. Processing function: confirmed by water quality correlation.*

**CAVEWARDEN**
Medium hexapod, 1.2–1.6m body. Six limbs with composite grip-pads that anchor to any surface including ceilings. Pale grey with faint warm amber banding. Eyes vestigial and non-functional — navigates by dense chemoreceptor array. Composite dorsal plating. Processes contaminated cave substrate through underside contact during resting periods.

Most frequently encountered Ashen fauna. Does not flee humans. Does not approach them. Continues what it was doing with complete indifference to human presence.

Southern Reaches oral tradition: *"the cave's own creature."* A functional heuristic has developed — if Cavewarden is present and comfortable, the air is breathable and the water is clean. This heuristic is accurate.

*Not aggressive under any documented circumstances. If physically obstructed, will wait with apparent patience until the obstruction moves. Documented waiting over four hours for a researcher who sat in its path to finish taking notes.*

**Inter-connections:** Cavewarden occupies cave surfaces while Seepworm processes the groundwater in the same systems — different contamination fractions in the same environment. Siltdrifter occupies underground river channels in caves Cavewarden inhabits on surfaces. Southern Reaches communities familiar with Cavewarden have developed practical cave navigation skills from observing its route preferences.

Salvage: composite dorsal plating, chemoreceptor array (chemical gradient sensitivity exceeds any known instrument — significant Veinborn interest), processing tissue from underside.

*Documentation status: Well-documented by Southern Reaches communities. Behavior cycle: understood empirically. Internal processing: examined by Veinborn with cave access.*

---

**SILTDRIFTER**
Elongated fish-form, 80cm–1.4m. Compressed laterally for narrow channel navigation. Pale white to faint blue-grey. No functional eyes — fully atrophied. Navigation by lateral line system: simultaneous detection of water movement, pressure changes, and chemical gradients. Groups of 10–20 occupy specific channel sections, spacing themselves to maximize total flow processed.

The cleanliness of natural spring water in Southern Reaches and Ridgeback foothills is substantially attributable to Siltdrifter populations upstream. Communities know their spring water tastes different from other sources — cleaner, with a faint mineral quality. The mineral quality is Siltdrifter processing residue.

Occasionally visible at cave spring outlets — a cluster of pale shapes in dark water, almost motionless against current, then gone as they sense the pressure change indicating open space ahead.

*Retreats from significant water disturbance. Significant flow disruption causes population relocation downstream over days to weeks.*

**Inter-connections:** Siltdrifter underground rivers connect zones between Tunnelworm burrows and surface spring outlets. Seepworm processes rock-contact water before it reaches Siltdrifter channels — serial processing of the same water through multiple Ashen lineage fauna before it surfaces. Southern Reaches communities benefit from this chain without awareness of any of its components.

Salvage: processing gill array, lateral line sensing organ (Veinborn interest), body tissue (minimal structural value).

*Documentation status: Spring outlet sightings documented for generations. Underground behavior: observed in accessible cave river systems. Deep channel populations: unobserved.*

---

**STONEPULSE**
Sedentary disc-form, 40–90cm diameter. Attaches to cave walls and underground chamber surfaces during early life and does not relocate. Surface texture indistinguishable from surrounding rock — same color, roughness, temperature. Produces no movement, sound, or light during normal operation. A cave containing fifty Stonepulse individuals appears to contain none.

Processes contamination through direct rock contact — absorbs compounds that have migrated into the rock matrix itself. Heavy metals, ancient industrial contamination that mineralized into stone over centuries. Processes what nothing else reaches.

Bioluminescent only during reproductive period — once per decade approximately, a slow pulse of deep amber light from the disc center, sustained for several hours. Southern Reaches communities that have explored deep enough encounter this: *"the caves breathe amber, slowly, once in a while."* The source has never been identified by any human faction.

*No defensive capability. No mobility. Removal from substrate causes gradual death over weeks.*

**Inter-connections:** Processes rock matrix contamination that Seepworm, Siltdrifter, and Tunnelworm cannot access — the deepest layer of Ashen lineage contamination removal. The amber pulse events are the only visible sign of Ashen lineage activity that Southern Reaches oral tradition has preserved — all other fauna in this lineage are effectively invisible to human observation.

Salvage: surface composite from deceased individuals — extremely hard, similar to processed stone, low value due to identification difficulty. Processing tissue degrades rapidly once removed from rock contact.

*Documentation status: Amber pulse phenomenon documented in oral tradition. Physical specimen identified only by researchers who knew what to look for. Distribution: may be present in any sufficiently deep cave system in the Known World.*
